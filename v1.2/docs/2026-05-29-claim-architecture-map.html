How to read this: left-to-right is the lifecycle of one claim — authored through a constrained questionnaire (①), stored & evaluated as a DAG of premises→operations→statistics→logic-gates (②, live today), then graded for strength and placed in the spatial corpus (③). The single biggest insight: the strength grade is not a new system — it is a bottom-up fold over the DAG that already exists, using provenance_state + evidence_class + materialization drift as leaf strengths and the AND/OR/NOT gates as the combination rule. A claim is exactly as strong as its weakest verifiable input allows.
