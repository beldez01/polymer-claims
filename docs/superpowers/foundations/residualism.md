# Logical Residualism: Instrumenting the Incompleteness of Inquiry

*An internal white paper from the polymer-claims project*

## Abstract

Logical residualism is a post-positivist philosophy of science built from, and operationalized inside, a compiler-and-runtime that licenses scientific claims only when two independent computational implementations agree. Its core observation is that every act of inquiry on a complex system must *cut* — fix a scale, a measurand, a set of operations, a vocabulary — and that the cut always leaves a residue. We make four claims. Every verificationist cut captures only a proper subset of what is real (R1). The residue it leaves is not noise or error but structured, constitutive, and informative (R2). An iterating, accumulating program of inquiry genuinely converts residue into licensed claim over time (R3). And — the load-bearing commitment — that conversion asymptotes to a positive floor, not to zero (R4): the map provably never equals the territory. A mature science is therefore one that has instrumented its own incompleteness rather than one that pretends to closure. We locate residualism's intellectual neighbors, argue R4 from converging independent grounds, and show how each thesis compiles into a running data structure or verdict.

## Framing: The Four Theses

This paper develops a position we call **logical residualism**, or residualism for short. It is not a revival of logical positivism; it inherits its dynamics from Peircean convergence, Popperian falsification, and Duhem–Quine holism, and it makes two narrow, load-bearing novel moves: it promotes the residue of inquiry from byproduct to a first-class, operationalized object, and it commits to a positive floor.

Read R4 first, because everything else is in its service:

- **(R4) The conversion of residue into captured claim asymptotes to a positive floor, not to zero.** The map provably falls short of the territory — not as a confession of immaturity but as a permanent structural feature of any finite system trained on a sufficiently complex one. A mature science is one that has instrumented its own incompleteness.

The other three theses set R4 up:

- **(R1)** Every verificationist cut on a complex system captures only a proper subset of what is real; a residue is always left on the table.
- **(R2)** That residue is constitutive, structured, and informative — shaped by the frame that excludes it — not error or noise.
- **(R3)** The residue is not fixed; an iterating, accumulating program of inquiry progressively converts residue into licensed claim, though non-monotonically.

The foreground claim, and the one most exposed to attack, is R4. We do not stipulate the floor; we earn it, domain by domain, from converging independent grounds. What follows builds the position, clears it of the verificationism it is mistaken for, situates it against the convergence tradition it quarrels with, makes the positive-floor case at length, specifies the iteration engine that drives R3, develops the methodological turn of *instrumenting* incompleteness, shows how each thesis is compiled into the claims engine, and finally absorbs the strongest objections and states the forward program.

## 1. The Position

Begin with the cell. A single eukaryotic cell is a continuous-state, thermodynamically open, self-producing system whose relevant molecular configurations number, on Walter Elsasser's estimate, far beyond 10^100 — orders of magnitude past the particle count of the observable universe (*Reflections on a Theory of Organisms*, 1987). No instrument, no model, no finite battery of measurements resolves all of it. Every act of inquiry must therefore *cut*: fix a scale, a measurand, a set of operations, a vocabulary, and read off what that choice makes legible. **Logical residualism is the thesis that the cut is real, that it always leaves something behind, that what it leaves behind is structured and informative, and — the load-bearing commitment — that no convergent program of cuts ever drives the leftover to zero.** The map provably falls short of the territory not as a confession of immaturity but as a permanent structural feature of any finite system trained on a sufficiently complex one (Korzybski's "a map is not the territory," *Science and Sanity*, 1933, here promoted from slogan to research program).

We call the leftover the **residue**, and define it precisely. Let a *verificationist cut* be any procedure that licenses claims about a target system by fixing a frame — a scale, an operationalization, an instrument model, a statistical commitment, and a representational vocabulary. The residue of a cut is the portion of the target that the frame does not and cannot conceptualize *under that frame*: what falls outside the homomorphism. This is not error (a mismeasurement, correctable by better instruments), not noise (stochastic nuisance, suppressible by averaging), and not yet-undefined vagueness alone. Following the representational theory of measurement (Krantz, Luce, Suppes & Tversky, *Foundations of Measurement*, 1971), every measurement is a *homomorphism* — structure-preserving but many-to-one — so the numerical image is provably a proper compression of the empirical structure. The residue is what the compression discards, and its *shape* is fixed by the frame's own boundary, which is why it carries information about that boundary rather than being random. Theodor Adorno's "objects do not go into their concepts without leaving a remainder" (*Negative Dialectics*, 1966) is the closest literal precedent for this constitutive reading; Wilfrid Sellars's *Myth of the Given* (*Empiricism and the Philosophy of Mind*, 1956) supplies its non-verificationist warrant, since every cut is conceptually constituted and so no frame-neutral, residue-free cut is even definable.

With those definitions fixed, the four theses can be stated in full.

**(R1) Every verificationist cut on a complex system captures only a proper subset of what is real.** A residue is always left on the table. This follows independently from measurement homomorphism (the gap is a theorem, not a contingency), from the conceptual ladenness of all observation (Sellars), and from the historical near-dilemma that killed strong verificationism: any cut sharp enough to be conclusive cannot capture unrestricted lawlike content, while any cut loose enough to admit that content cannot exclude residue (Hempel, "Problems and Changes in the Empiricist Criterion of Meaning," 1950).

**(R2) The residue is constitutive, structured, and informative — not error or noise.** It is shaped by the frame that excludes it and can be read for what that frame missed. Kuhn loss — content a superseded paradigm solved and its successor discarded (Heinz Post, 1971; Kuhn, *Structure*, 1962) — is the canonical historical exhibit: a structured, recoverable leftover, not a deleted mistake.

**(R3) The residue is not fixed.** An iterating, accumulating program of inquiry progressively converts residue into licensed claim. This is Hasok Chang's *epistemic iteration* (*Inventing Temperature*, 2004) — bootstrapping a better frame from a provisional one without a final foundation — and Nowak's *concretization* (relaxing idealizing assumptions one at a time, *The Structure of Idealization*, 1980). Conversion is real, but non-monotone: revolutions lose as they gain, so what is preserved is the *record*, not a monotonically growing truth-set.

**(R4) The conversion asymptotes to a positive floor, not to zero.** This is the crux. A mature science is one that has *instrumented its own incompleteness* rather than one that pretends to closure. The floor is not asserted; it is argued from converging independent grounds — Shannon's rate-distortion converse (zero distortion of a continuous, high-entropy source requires infinite rate, 1959); Elsasser's combinatorial immensity and Kauffman's biological non-ergodicity (the adjacent possible is unprestatable, Longo, Montévil & Kauffman, 2012); weak and asymptotic emergence (Bedau, 1997; Batterman, 2002); and Rescher's cognitive inexhaustibility (*The Limits of Science*, 1999), the closest card-carrying positive-floor realism in the literature.

**What residualism is and is not.** It is *not* logical positivism revived. The Vienna Circle made verification a theory of *meaning*; residualism's agreement gate is a *licensing* rule about epistemic warrant, and it pointedly refuses to call unlicensed or PENDING claims meaningless — which is precisely what immunizes it against the self-refutation that sank the verification principle (Ayer himself conceding, on Bryan Magee's *Men of Ideas*, BBC, 1978, that "nearly all of it was false"). It is *post-positivist*: Peircean in process (a self-correcting community of inquiry) but anti-Peircean in limit, since R4 denies the zero-residue convergence that the *canonical* reading of Peirce's "final opinion" (1878) suggests — though Peirce himself, with his fallibilism, tychism, and logic of vagueness, is genuinely ambivalent, and the clean zero-residue foil is really twentieth-century convergent realism (Putnam's no-miracles slogan, 1975; Boyd, 1984), whose deeper root is J. J. C. Smart's "cosmic coincidence" argument (1963). Residualism's two novel moves are narrow and load-bearing: it promotes the residue from byproduct to a *first-class, operationalized object* (the PENDING graveyard, the `duhem_underdetermined` verdict, the value-based defeat graph), and it commits to the positive floor.

One honest caution sets the tone for everything that follows: the floor must be *earned*, domain by domain, not borrowed. Global underdetermination — the strongest classical ally for R4 — belongs to the *later* Quine ("On Empirically Equivalent Systems of the World," *Erkenntnis*, 1975), not to the confirmation holism of "Two Dogmas" (1951); and it is an ally its own author half-recanted, wavering toward intertranslatability. We therefore do not write "provably never equals the territory" on Quinean grounds (cf. Laudan & Leplin, 1991). The proof, where there is one, comes from elsewhere — and building that case is the work of this paper. The first step is to be precise about the program residualism is built on the wreckage of, and does not belong to.

## 2. Against the Verificationist Cut

To see why the polymer engine is *post*-positivist rather than a positivism revived in software, we have to be precise about what the Vienna Circle actually claimed and how it actually failed.

The load-bearing thesis of logical positivism was not demarcation but **meaning**. On the verification principle a non-analytic sentence is cognitively significant if and only if it is, in some sense, verifiable in experience; the boundary between science and metaphysics fell out as a *corollary* of a theory of cognitive significance (Schlick, "Meaning and Verification," 1936). This is the crucial point residualism reacts against: the criterion governed what could be *said meaningfully*, not merely what could be *warranted*. The history is then a one-way liberalization, because the strong reading was immediately fatal. Schlick's "conclusive" verifiability gave way to Carnap's confirmability and reduction sentences ("Testability and Meaning," 1936–37), and to Ayer's "weak" verifiability (*Language, Truth and Logic*, 1936; 2nd ed. 1946). One should not caricature the Circle as monolithic: the left wing (Neurath, Carnap, Hahn — see the 1929 manifesto *Wissenschaftliche Weltauffassung*) already held revisable, anti-foundationalist views — Neurath's *Protokollsätze* (1932) denies any final observation base, and the boat that bears his name is rebuilt plank by plank at sea.

The program collapsed along three seams, and each seam is quietly residualism's evidence.

**Collapse 1 — self-refutation.** The verification principle is itself neither analytic nor empirically verifiable, so by its own lights it is cognitively meaningless. The standard escape — reclassifying it as a proposal or convention — concedes it has no truth-evaluable content. Ayer effectively conceded the whole enterprise on Bryan Magee's BBC series *Men of Ideas* (1978), allowing that "nearly all of it was false."

**Collapse 2 — universal and lawlike content.** No finite body of observation entails an unrestricted universal generalization, so strong verifiability declares the core content of science — "all copper conducts" — meaningless (and asymmetrically licenses existential statements while withholding their negations). This is the decisive structural failure, and Hume's problem of induction blocks every rescue: finite confirmation never closes a universal. Popper built his alternative on exactly this asymmetry (*Logik der Forschung*, 1934 / *The Logic of Scientific Discovery*, 1959): one counter-instance can falsify what no number of instances can verify.

**Collapse 3 — instability of the criterion.** Hempel's survey ("Problems and Changes in the Empiricist Criterion of Meaning," 1950) showed each repaired criterion was either too strong (killing laws and theoretical terms) or too weak (admitting anything). Alonzo Church's 1949 review (*Journal of Symbolic Logic*) gave the clean formal result: on Ayer's weak criterion, for any sentence *S* one can construct an auxiliary so that *S* helps entail an observation the auxiliary alone does not — so "the Absolute is lazy" qualifies. The boundary the criterion existed to draw could not be drawn.

Read together, these are not three contingent embarrassments but a single near-formal **dilemma about cuts**: any criterion strong enough to be a clean cut cannot capture lawlike content, and any criterion weak enough to admit that content cannot exclude residue. There is no verificationist cut without residue. That is a structural warrant for R1, and it explains why the residue is not noise — it is precisely the projective, lawlike surplus that defines science (R2).

Here the historical reframe must be sharp, because the obvious objection is that the polymer engine *is* a verificationist cut: two independent implementations must agree, so it inherits the demarcation and triviality troubles. The answer is to make one distinction load-bearing rather than rhetorical. **The agreement gate licenses; it does not mean.** It confers epistemic warrant on a claim; it makes no pronouncement about the cognitive significance of what it withholds a license from. An unlicensed claim is not declared meaningless — it is parked, as PENDING or `duhem_underdetermined`, as structured residue retained for further inquiry. This is the move that killed positivism reversed: the gate is a procedural acceptance rule, not a self-applying criterion of meaning, so it is not refuted by being itself unlicensable. Collapse 1 does not bite.

What replaces verification-as-meaning is the post-positivist epistemology assembled by Quine and Popper, and it is Duhem–Quine holism, not positivist atomism. Quine's "Two Dogmas of Empiricism" (1951) removed verificationism's precondition: meaning-by-verification presupposes that individual statements carry individual empirical content, but on confirmation holism statements "face the tribunal of sense experience not individually but only as a corporate body." Two precisions matter. First, "Two Dogmas" establishes *confirmation holism*; the stronger thesis that empirically equivalent total theories diverge under *all possible* evidence is a later and more equivocal Quinean doctrine ("On the Reasons for Indeterminacy of Translation," 1970; "On Empirically Equivalent Systems of the World," 1975) — and in 1975 Quine himself wavered toward intertranslatability, the "ecumenical versus sectarian" hesitation. We return to the consequences of this in Section 3; here it suffices that we do not rest any "provably never equals the territory" claim on Quine. Second, the `duhem_underdetermined` label carries an irony worth stating plainly: Duhem expressly confined his holism to theoretical physics and excluded physiology and the life sciences (*La Théorie physique*, 1906/1914). On a living-cell claim Duhem himself would deny his thesis applies; the cell-level verdict is *Quinean globalization*, not Duhem — the project's central biological target is exactly the domain Duhem fenced off. The engine is Popperian where it accumulates corroborated-but-revisable conjectures and decisive where a claim is defeated; it is Quinean–holist where it refuses the positivist demand for a determinate verification value. It verifies nothing about meaning. It licenses, defeats, and remembers.

## 3. The Convergence Tradition and the Fork

Residualism is a child of the convergence tradition, and its decisive claim is a quarrel with that tradition's parent. Begin with Peirce, whose 1878 formula remains the canonical statement of truth-as-convergence: truth is "the opinion which is fated to be ultimately agreed to by all who investigate," and the real is the object of that opinion ("How to Make Our Ideas Clear," 1878). Read flatly, this is an asymptote-to-zero doctrine: pursue any question far enough and inquiry settles it, so the residue on that question vanishes at the ideal limit. The air-gapped agreement gate of polymer-claims is a procedural cash-out of exactly Peirce's mechanism — the "force outside themselves" that drags antagonistic investigators toward one conclusion — and the self-correcting corpus is his community of inquiry made into running code. On the dynamics of R3, residualism is straightforwardly Peircean.

But Peirce is not the clean zero-residue foil he is often made into, and we should resist conscripting him as one. His fallibilism, his tychism (absolute chance is real), his synechism (reality is a continuum), and his logic of vagueness all commit him to ineliminable indeterminacy. The 1878 doctrine quantifies over questions *actually investigated*; the subjunctive "would be agreed" of his later "Truth and Falsity and Error" (1901, CP 5.565) plus genuine vagueness leave ample room for a permanent residue of unposed questions and unresolved continua. Catherine Legg's reading of Peircean truth as a *limit concept* — an asymptote actual inquiry need never reach ("Charles Peirce's Limit Concept of Truth," *Philosophy Compass* 9(3), 2014) — separates "truth as regulative target" from "truth as ever attained," precisely the distinction residualism needs. Peirce is an ancestor of both sides of the fork, not a member of the opposing camp.

The genuinely sharp zero-residue position is twentieth-century convergent realism. Its engine is the no-miracles argument, whose pedigree is older and broader than the slogan usually credited to it. The "cosmic coincidence" intuition originates with J. J. C. Smart (*Philosophy and Scientific Realism*, 1963); Putnam supplies the canonical phrasing — realism is "the only philosophy that does not make the success of science a miracle" ("What Is Mathematical Truth?", in *Mathematics, Matter and Method*, 1975); and van Fraassen later dubbed it "the ultimate argument" (*The Scientific Image*, 1980). Boyd then gives the abductive defense: the instrumental reliability of scientific method is best explained by the approximate truth of background theories, progressively refined toward truth ("The Current Status of Scientific Realism," in Leplin, ed., *Scientific Realism*, 1984). This family holds, in effect, that residue(t) → 0: success tracks approximate truth, and theories close on the territory across change.

The standing challenge to that optimism is Laudan's pessimistic meta-induction ("A Confutation of Convergent Realism," *Philosophy of Science* 48(1), 1981): many empirically successful past theories are now judged false and non-referring, so success warrants no inference to truth, and no monotone convergence is demonstrated. Residualism's relation to Laudan is subtle and load-bearing. It *concedes the history* — accumulation is not clean — but rejects his interpretation of the discards. For Laudan, superseded content is error, to be forgotten. For residualism's R2, some of it is constitutive, structured residue, to be instrumented. This is the thesis Laudan most directly denies, and it is the one the PENDING graveyard and the `duhem_underdetermined` verdict are built to defend: a claim that fails the agreement gate is un-licensed and retained, not erased as falsehood.

Here is the fork. Convergent realism and residualism agree on the dynamics and differ only on the limit. Convergent realism conjectures lim(residue) = 0 — at the ideal end of inquiry the final opinion settles every well-posed question and the map equals the territory. Residualism's R4 conjectures lim(residue) = F > 0 — a positive, ineliminable floor remains no matter how long inquiry runs. The whole position turns on earning F > 0 rather than stipulating it, and on doing so honestly, which means refusing two tempting but broken supports.

The first temptation is to lean on Quine for a *proven* floor. The strongest classical ally for R4 is global underdetermination: distinct total theories empirically equivalent under all possible evidence, hence evidentially indistinguishable territories. But, as Section 2 anticipated, this is not the holism of "Two Dogmas." The strong claim is the later Quine — "On the Reasons for Indeterminacy of Translation" (1970) and especially "On Empirically Equivalent Systems of the World" (1975) — and even there Quine wavered, drifting toward intertranslatability of apparent rivals ("ecumenical" versus "sectarian"). R4's strongest classical ally is thus a thesis its own author half-recanted, and Laudan and Leplin ("Empirical Equivalence and Underdetermination," *J. Phil.* 88, 1991) argue empirical equivalence is neither necessary nor sufficient for evidential equivalence. So the floor cannot be advertised as *provably* never equal to the territory on Quinean grounds; on this lineage it is plausible, not proven. The second temptation is Popper's verisimilitude as a metric of "approach to a floor above zero." That resource is a known dead end: Miller and Tichý independently proved (1974) that Popper's definitions order only true theories and cannot compare two false ones — the very case they were meant to handle. R4 must be grounded elsewhere — complexity, emergence, information-theoretic and formal limits — never in truthlikeness arithmetic.

Why is the floor view the honest one, and why is it not defeatism? Defeatism says inquiry is futile and the residue is darkness. Residualism says the opposite on both counts: R3 is real — an iterating program genuinely converts residue into licensed claim — and the floor is *instrumented*, made a first-class, measured object rather than a confession. The distinction from Popper sharpens here. Popper's is an *epistemic* floor: the target stays full truth, we merely never know we have arrived. Residualism's is, more ambitiously, a *structural* floor: there is residue left in the limit, and a mature science is one that has named, located, and tracked it. That is the difference between pretending to a closure the system's own structure forbids and building the instrument that registers, as data, exactly where capture fails. The next section earns that structural floor.

## 4. The Case for a Positive Floor

The positive-floor commitment (R4) is the paper's most exposed claim, so it must be *earned* rather than stipulated. The honest target is not the weak Popperian thesis that residue is never *known* to reach zero (a fact about our certainty), but the strong thesis that residue is bounded away from zero by the structure of the inquiry and its object. No single argument secures this. The case is instead *cumulative and overdetermined*: several mutually independent mechanisms — formal, metrological, nomological, biological, and tacit — each force a remainder, and in each the remainder is a determinate, structured object rather than noise (R2). What follows is the union of these floors. The position survives even if any one of them fails.

**The formal floor (provable, for self-describing systems).** Gödel (1931) and Tarski (1936) give the only strictly *provable* residue in residualism's neighborhood. Any consistent, recursively axiomatized theory adequate for arithmetic contains a true sentence it cannot prove, and cannot define its own truth predicate; the residue is *constructible* — a specific diagonal sentence — not vague. Crucially for R2, this is the opposite of noise: it is a theorem. Accumulation does not escape it: adjoining the Gödel sentence as a new axiom yields a stronger system with its *own* fresh undecidable sentence, so a capture program that licenses claims (adds axioms) shrinks local residue yet provably regenerates it — an asymptote to a floor, not to zero. One caution we enforce throughout: Chaitin's halting probability Ω shows that *some* mathematical facts are algorithmically *incompressible* — irreducible information not derivable from any shorter axiom set (Chaitin 2006) — but incompressibility of one real does not by itself establish that undecidable sentences have *positive density*. For the stronger "floor with mass" claim one should cite density results directly: Calude and Jürgensen show that, under a natural measure, the proportion of true-but-unprovable sentences has positive upper density — unprovability is the norm, provability the rare case. The standing liability is category transfer: these govern *formal* provability, and applying them to an empirical cut on a cell is analogy until the verification gate is itself modeled as a consistent, self-referential formal system.

**The metrological floor.** Measurement supplies a theorem-grade gap that needs no analogy. On the representational theory (Krantz, Luce, Suppes & Tversky 1971), a measurement is a *homomorphism* — structure-preserving but many-to-one — fixed only up to permissible transformations; the numerical image is therefore a proper compression of the empirical structure, so model ≠ territory by construction. Operationalism is the cautionary dual: Bridgman's (1927) attempt to make a concept *equal* one operation — a zero-residue cut — collapsed precisely because the concept always exceeds the operation, and Hempel (1966) showed that denying the residue forces an "intolerable" proliferation of concepts. Cronbach and Meehl (1955) then *institutionalized* the remainder: construct validity concedes up front that no operationalization exhausts a construct and relocates meaning into an open, revisable nomological net. Tal's model-based account (2012; SEP) locates the floor sharply: part of measurement uncertainty derives not from instrument error but from *definitional* vagueness in the measurand itself, which a perfect instrument cannot remove. Mature metrology already keeps an uncertainty budget — residue as a first-class object.

**The nomological floor.** Cartwright's dappled world (1999) gives the floor a metaphysical, not merely epistemic, footing: fundamental laws hold only *ceteris paribus*, inside engineered "nomological machines," so open, un-shielded systems — the living cell paradigmatically — are governed by no single covering law and host indefinitely many further machines. Weisberg's (2007) taxonomy supplies the lever: a floor exists *iff* the idealization is non-Galilean. Nowak's (1980) concretization — sequentially relaxing explicit idealizing assumptions — is the cleanest model of *converting* residue to claim (R3) and shows the residue is a structured ledger (R2); but read straight it is *Galilean*, aiming at completeness, so residualism cannot rest R4 on Nowak. The floor comes from Cartwright plus Weisberg's *multiple-models* idealization, whose representational ideal explicitly is not a single complete model.

**The biological floor (the load-bearing tier).** Here precision matters most. The strongest temptation is Rosen's (1991) claim that an organism, being closed to efficient causation, possesses a *non-simulable* model — no Turing machine computes its modeling relation. Presented in strongest form this is a genuine in-principle floor; but it is contested mathematics (Mossio, Longo & Stewart 2009 expressed closure to efficient causation in λ-calculus; Letelier, Cárdenas et al. 2010 report computable realizations), and it is self-undermining for a *computational* program — if cells were strictly non-computable, two agreeing implementations could never license any cell claim. Residualism should therefore cite Rosen only for the conceptual frame ("no largest model"; residue as constitutive of life's logic) and rest the actual no-go on two *weaker, computation-compatible* arguments. Elsasser's (1987/1998) cardinality floor: the distinguishable molecular configurations of even one cell yield "immense numbers" (>10^100, far exceeding the particles in the universe), so reductive statistics can only ever sample a measure-zero slice — residue is guaranteed by combinatorics, no anti-computationalism required. Kauffman's temporal floor (Longo, Montévil & Kauffman 2012): the biosphere is radically non-ergodic and its "adjacent possible" is unprestatable, so no entailing law fixes its phase space; the target of inquiry is itself a moving frontier. Neither claims to violate physics, which is exactly why they are the safe load-bearers.

**The tacit floor.** Finally, Polanyi (1966) gives a knower-level floor: every explicit articulation is integrated *from* unspecifiable subsidiary particulars, so explicitation *relocates* the tacit rather than draining it — zero residue is not merely unachieved but structurally incoherent. Collins (2010) makes this non-mystical by partitioning it: relational tacit knowledge is convertible (R3 is real), but collective tacit knowledge "even in principle" resists mechanization — and an air-gapped, two-implementation gate *is* a mechanical procedure, so for any such verifier the floor's lower bound is the collective-tacit, embodied dimension of the knowing community. McClintock's "feeling for the organism" (Keller 1983) shows this floor is *generative*, not inert.

**The shape of the cumulative argument.** Each tier is individually contestable, and a critic can attack any one (the formal results are about provability, not cells; Cartwright's gap is static, not asymptotic; Rosen's theorem is disputed). But they fail *independently* and bottom out in different features of the world — self-reference, compression, law-coverage, cardinality, non-ergodicity, tacit integration. That an irreducible remainder is forced by arguments this heterogeneous, from traditions this hostile to one another, is itself the evidence that the floor is not a quirk of one formalism. And in every case the residue is the same *kind* of thing: a constructible sentence, an incompressible bit, an explicit idealizing assumption, a measure-zero sample, an unprestatable frontier, an unspecifiable subsidiary. The floor is real because it is overdetermined; it is informative (R2) because, wherever it appears, it is structured. A mature science does not abolish it — it instruments it. But first it must be *converted* where conversion is possible, and that is the dynamics R3 names.

## 5. The Iteration Engine

If R1 says every cut leaves a residue and R2 says that residue is structured, R3 is the claim that the residue is not static: an iterating, accumulating program of inquiry *converts* residue into captured claim over time. The question this section answers is mechanical — by what dynamics? — and the answer has two complementary engines in the literature, one epistemological (Hasok Chang's epistemic iteration) and one formal (Leszek Nowak's concretization). Together they specify how conversion happens step by step; neither, on its own, delivers R4's positive floor, and saying exactly why is what keeps residualism honest.

Start with Chang. In *Inventing Temperature* (2004), epistemic iteration is "a process in which successive stages of knowledge, each building on the preceding one, are created in order to enhance the achievement of certain epistemic goals." The crucial structural fact is that inquiry has no indubitable foundation to stand on: "we start from where we stand. The foundation is never indubitable; the foundation is provisional." Chang's worked case is thermometry — validating a thermometer presupposes knowing temperature, which presupposes a trusted thermometer — and iteration breaks the circle not by finding a neutral ground but by accepting a provisional instrument, using it, and correcting it with its own outputs. He distinguishes two modes (chs. 5–6): *enrichment*, where the inherited system is refined and its epistemic virtues enhanced without being negated, and *self-correction*, where the system's content is actually altered by inquiry grounded in itself. This is a progressive coherentism, explicitly Neurathian — rebuilding the boat plank by plank — not foundationalism.

This is, almost verbatim, the temporal dynamics of the polymer engine. The accumulating claims universe is the provisional corpus we stand on; the air-gapped agreement gate plus the defeat graph are the self-correction mechanism that "can put any claim in jeopardy, though not all at once." Kevin Elliott's (2012) refinement is useful here because it cleaves the loop along the same seam the engine does: *epistemic* iteration (the progressive alteration of knowledge claims — corpus growth) is distinct from *methodological* iteration (the interplay between modes of research practice — the dual-implementation requirement). The engine runs both at once.

But Chang issues a warning residualism must absorb rather than wave away. For Chang, convergence is *not* truth. Where iteration stabilizes, that stability is read as virtue-assessed progress (accuracy, scope, fruitfulness), never as verified correspondence; iteration can also dead-end and force a restart from a new provisional base. In his mature view (*Realism for Realistic People*, 2022) he replaces correspondence-truth with "operational coherence" outright. The consequence: Chang would resist R4's "the map never equals the territory" phrasing as still too correspondence-flavored, because on his account there is no single completable map to fall short of. What he gives instead is a floor of a *different shape* — *permanent pluralism* (*Is Water H₂O?*, 2012): multiple incommensurable systems of practice over one domain should be maintained indefinitely because plurality is productive, not a defect awaiting elimination. The Chemical Revolution, on his reading, converged prematurely. So residualism inherits from Chang an anti-closure commitment, but it is anti-collapse-to-one rather than non-zero-distance-to-territory. The honest move is to treat permanent pluralism as a sibling floor: the `duhem_underdetermined` verdict and PENDING graveyard are the engine *refusing premature consensus*, keeping several captures alive — Chang's normative pluralism operationalized inside a verification pipeline.

Nowak supplies the missing quantitative grammar. In *The Structure of Idealization* (1980), a law is stated as an idealizational statement holding under explicit counterfactual conditions — secondary factors set to zero. Inquiry then proceeds by *concretization*: relaxing those assumptions one at a time, each adding a correction term, until in the limit one reaches a factual statement about the concrete object. This is the sharpest available model of R3. The residue here is not noise but a *named, ranked ledger* — the explicit set of idealizing assumptions — so concretization is an auditable, step-by-step conversion of residue into corrected claim, which independently reinforces R2.

And here is the load-bearing caveat, already flagged in Section 4. Read straight, Nowak *threatens* R4. A program of full concretization aspires to asymptote to the concrete truth — zero residue. This is what Weisberg (2007) calls *Galilean* idealization, whose representational ideal is completeness. Residualism therefore cannot rest its floor on Nowak. The floor must come from the target being *non-Galilean*. Weisberg's other categories supply the alternative: *minimalist* idealization (keep only core difference-makers) and especially *multiple-models* idealization, which accepts a permanent plurality of incompatible models because no single one can be maximally accurate, simple, and general at once. Cartwright's dappled world (1999) gives the metaphysical reason to think living systems are exactly this: open, capacity-laden systems governed by no single covering law, hosting indefinitely many further local "nomological machines." The temporal picture, then, is precise: concretization (Nowak) is the *engine* of conversion; whether that engine asymptotes to zero or to a positive floor depends entirely on whether the target is Galilean. For the cell, the bet — to be argued, not assumed — is that it is constitutively dappled, so de-idealization never converges to completeness.

The synthesis: iteration genuinely converts residue (Chang's enrichment/self-correction, Nowak's relaxation of assumptions), and it does so monotonically *per question*. What it never does is close the system. Residualism's distinctive addition over both is promoting the leftover to a first-class, measured object — the residue ledger, the PENDING graveyard, the underdetermination verdict — which Chang gestures at normatively and Nowak treats as a vanishing remainder, but which neither operationalizes. That promotion is itself a method, and it has a lineage of its own.

## 6. Instrumenting Incompleteness

The methodological turn that separates residualism from every philosophy of science it borrows from is a verb: *instrument*. A mature science does not merely tolerate what it cannot yet say; it builds, as a first-class artifact, an apparatus that registers *that*, *where*, and *why* its capture fails. The residue is not the silence after the last licensed claim. It is a structured object with its own data — the PENDING graveyard, the `duhem_underdetermined` verdict, the defeat graph — that a science maintains with the same rigor it brings to its positive results. This is the operational content of R2.

The lineage for this move is a *negative-space* tradition that has always treated the boundary of the sayable as itself a source of knowledge. Its oldest stratum is apophatic: Pseudo-Dionysius's *Mystical Theology* (c. 500 CE) proceeds by negation, knowing its object by articulating what it is *not* and treating the limit — the "divine darkness" — as super-luminous rather than as mere ignorance. Nicholas of Cusa's *De Docta Ignorantia* (1440) sharpens this into a method: the height of wisdom is "learned ignorance," a *precise* grasp of the boundary one cannot cross, the infinite approached as a polygon approaches a circle, never coinciding. Keats's "negative capability" — the 1817 letter's capacity for "being in uncertainties, mysteries, doubts, without any irritable reaching after fact and reason" — supplies the *posture* the apparatus institutionalizes: dwelling productively in the unresolved rather than forcing premature closure. These are stances and arguments, not theorems, and we bill them as such; their contribution is to the *verb* of R4, the disciplined occupancy of the gap.

What converts posture into proof is the formal-limits cluster, and here precision is non-negotiable. Gödel's First Incompleteness Theorem (1931) shows that any consistent, recursively axiomatized theory strong enough for arithmetic contains a sentence *G* that is true in the standard model yet unprovable in the theory. The decisive feature for residualism is not that *G* exists but that *G* is *constructible* — a specific self-referential sentence built via the diagonal lemma. The residue is a theorem, not noise: the cleanest possible vindication of R2. Equally important is the dynamic structure. Add *G* as a new axiom and the strengthened theory has its own fresh Gödel sentence *G′*. Licensing claims is like adding axioms; accumulation shrinks the *local* residue yet provably regenerates a new one at every stage. This is the technical mirror of R3 conjoined with R4: a captured fraction that rises monotonically and never reaches totality.

But two over-claims must be refused. First, Gödel's residue is, under most natural measures, *thin* — a single undecidable sentence and its iterates are consistent with the captured fraction approaching one. The floor's *mass* cannot be sourced from bare incompleteness. Gregory Chaitin's halting probability Ω (*Meta Math!*, 2005; 2006) does establish something stronger: the bits of Ω are algorithmically incompressible — well-defined mathematical facts derivable from no axiom system shorter than themselves. That is the right citation for irreducible, non-derivable *information*. It is *not*, however, a license to assert a "positive-density floor" of undecidable sentences. Incompressibility of one real does not entail positive density of the undecidable set under a natural measure; that is a separate, measure-dependent claim, and if a density result is wanted it should be sourced from work specifically on the proportion of true-but-unprovable statements (e.g., Calude and Jürgensen), not inferred from Ω. Residualism should say exactly what Ω gives — irreducible information — and no more.

Second, Tarski's undefinability theorem (1933/36) cuts both ways and must be handled honestly. No consistent language meeting modest conditions contains its own total truth predicate; "true-in-*L*" is definable only in a richer metalanguage *L′*. Read carelessly this supports R4; read carefully it threatens it, because the truth *is* recoverable — one level up. The floor is therefore not a fixed quantum of forever-dark content but a *structural* impossibility: no system captures its own total truth, and the metalanguage hierarchy never terminates. The residue is relocated, never abolished. That is a subtler and more defensible thesis than "a region of permanent darkness," and residualism should stake its claim on the non-termination of the hierarchy rather than on any single sealed-off content.

Mysterianism must be cited with deliberate restraint. Colin McGinn's "cognitive closure" (*Mind*, 1989) — a mind whose concept-forming procedures cannot reach a property, while knowing *that* and roughly *why* the problem is closed — is a near-perfect model of instrumented incompleteness: knowing the shape of what one cannot know. But it is a claim about contingent *human* faculties, not about the territory; Dennett dismisses it as a "cop-out," and Owen Flanagan coined "new mysterians" pejoratively (Thomas Nagel, grouped under the label for the irreducible "what-it-is-like" residue of *What Is It Like to Be a Bat?*, 1974, holds a more guarded view than McGinn and did not self-describe this way). Lean on McGinn for the *verb* — mapping the boundary without claiming the missed content — and not as evidence that the boundary is metaphysically permanent.

The load-bearing caveat is the same one the apophatic and formal traditions both invite: every formal result here governs *formal systems* — provability, definability, compressibility. Transferring them to an empirical cut on a living cell is an analogy, not a deduction, unless the two-implementation agreement gate is itself modeled as a self-referential, consistent, recursively axiomatized system meeting the theorems' hypotheses. The honest framing is not that incompleteness *proves* an empirical floor but that it furnishes the template: any system honest enough to describe its own limits necessarily has them, and the mature response is to instrument the boundary rather than pretend to closure. We turn now to how that template is compiled into running code.

## 7. Residualism Operationalized: The Claims Engine

Residualism is not only a set of theses; in polymer-claims each thesis is compiled into a data structure or a verdict. The position earns its keep by being *runnable*. What follows maps R1–R4 onto the mechanisms that instantiate them.

**The agreement gate: Peircean convergence as a decision procedure.** A claim is licensed only when two independently built computational implementations agree. This is Peirce's "opinion which is fated to be ultimately agreed to by all who investigate" (1878) shrunk to an acceptance rule — the "force outside themselves" that drives antagonistic inquirers toward one conclusion. But the gate is best understood through robustness and multiple-determination rather than verification: Hacking's argument from coincidence (*Representing and Intervening*, 1983), Salmon's common-cause principle (1984, exemplified by Perrin's many independent determinations of Avogadro's number), and Wimsatt's invariance under independent processes (1981). Decisively, the gate *licenses*; it does not pronounce on meaning. A licensed claim is Dewey's "warranted assertibility" (*Logic: The Theory of Inquiry*, 1938) operationalized — a status conferred by surviving controlled inquiry, not a verdict of truth or cognitive significance. That single distinction is what keeps residualism clear of the self-refutation that sank verificationism: the gate makes no claim about the significance of what it withholds a license from.

**The defeat graph: Popper at corpus scale.** Licensed claims are corroborated conjectures, held defeasibly (Popper, *The Logic of Scientific Discovery*, 1959; *Conjectures and Refutations*, 1963). Inter-claim conflict is resolved not by proof but by value-based defeat with a grounded extension — formally, Bench-Capon's value-based argumentation frameworks (2003), in which defeat is relative to an audience's ordering over the values arguments promote. This is the exact structure of Kuhn's 1977 thesis that the five theory-choice values (accuracy, consistency, scope, simplicity, fruitfulness) underdetermine choice with no neutral algorithm; the engine does not eliminate the value judgment but makes it an explicit, audited parameter on which the extension is conditional. Two cautions. The graph must not lean on Popper's verisimilitude as a measure of "approach to truth" — Miller and Tichý (1974) proved that formalism orders only true theories. And the grounded extension is non-monotone: adding an argument flips statuses, mirroring Kuhn loss (Post 1971). The engine answers this by never deleting — a defeated claim is demoted, not erased — so what is monotone is the audit trail, not the licensed set.

**Blame-sets and the `duhem_underdetermined` verdict: instrumented underdetermination.** Each implementation is a whole bundle — substantive claim plus auxiliary assumptions plus instrument theory — so a disagreement indicts the corporate body without isolating the culprit. This is confirmation holism, which is all that "Two Dogmas of Empiricism" (1951) actually establishes. The named verdict refuses the positivist demand for a determinate accept/reject value and instead instruments the holist indeterminacy. Two honest caveats belong in the main text rather than a footnote, and they recur because they bind several mechanisms at once. First, the load-bearing premise for R4's floor is *global, contrastive* underdetermination — empirically equivalent total theories — which is the later Quine ("On the Reasons for Indeterminacy of Translation," 1970; "On Empirically Equivalent Systems of the World," 1975), and a doctrine Quine himself wavered on, drifting toward intertranslatability; Laudan and Leplin (1991) deny that empirical equivalence even entails evidential equivalence, so we do not assert "provably never equals the territory" on Quinean grounds. Second, the verdict's name is ironic: Duhem expressly restricted his holism to theoretical physics and excluded physiology and the life sciences — precisely the engine's central biological target. "Duhemian" here means the post-Quine generalized sense, and the irony is worth owning.

**The PENDING graveyard: the residue made visible.** A claim that passes one implementation but fails the gate is not falsified, only un-licensed. The graveyard is R2 rendered in software — the residue promoted from discarded byproduct to a first-class, structured, queryable object. What positivism threw out as meaningless metaphysics, residualism retains as Stegenga's (2009) "discordant" evidence: structured, informative, and method-diagnostic rather than noise.

**The expanding claims universe: epistemic iteration in running code.** The accumulating corpus is Chang's epistemic iteration (2004) mechanized — inquiry begins from a provisional, non-indubitable base and uses its own outputs to correct that base. It is a Neurathian boat rebuilt plank by plank, or, in Sellars's words, "a self-correcting enterprise which can put any claim in jeopardy, though not all at once" (*Empiricism and the Philosophy of Mind*, 1956, §38). Elliott's (2012) distinction maps directly: corpus growth is *epistemic* iteration, the dual-implementation gate is *methodological* iteration.

**Decorrelation: the residue reappears inside the gate.** The sharpest finding is that the residue does not stay outside the gate — it re-enters it. The air-gap secures *implementation* independence (distinct code, distinct authors) but not *conceptual* independence. If both implementations inherit the same operationalizations, ontology, and measurement conventions, their agreement tracks the shared frame, not the territory — the theory-neutral Given smuggled back in as a "neutral" scheme (Sellars 1956; Hanson 1958 and Kuhn 1962 on theory-ladenness). Longino (1990) shows convergence can certify a community's shared background assumptions; Cartwright warns that two re-derivations are "different ways of doing the same thing," epistemically weaker than two causally distinct instruments; Orzack and Sober (1993) note that a robust theorem is true only if at least one model is, so agreement among assumption-sharing models tracks the assumption. Correlated bias is, by construction, invisible to agreement. The residualist response is to reclassify the gate: it is a method-dependence detector, not a correspondence oracle, and the strength of any license is bounded by the breadth of independence actually sampled — ontology, operationalization, instrument model, statistical priors — which the engine must certify and log, not infer from code diversity alone. This is the bridge from philosophy to artifact: R1 lives in the gate's partiality, R2 in the graveyard, R3 in the iterating corpus, and R4 both in the graveyard that never empties and in the decorrelation residue the gate cannot certify away from within.

## 8. Objections, Replies, and the Research Program

A position is only as strong as the objections it can absorb without flinching. We take four.

**Objection 1: The positive floor is unfalsifiable, mysterian, or defeatist.** If residue asymptotes to a strictly positive floor *F* > 0, how could any finite run of inquiry ever refute that? And does naming a permanent reservoir of the uncaptured not amount to McGinn-style mysterianism — a "cop-out" dressed as humility?

Reply: R4 is not offered as an empirical prediction about a measured quantity converging to a number we can read off. It is a structural thesis with discharge conditions. It is *defeated* if a complex living system is exhibited whose complete behavior is captured by a finite, compressible covering law with no surviving method-dependent residue — exactly the closure the dappled-world tradition (Cartwright 1999) and rate-distortion theory (Shannon's converse coding theorem, 1959; Berger 1971) say is unavailable for a continuous, high-entropy source. The floor is therefore conditional and contestable, not self-sealing. As for mysterianism: residualism inverts McGinn. Mysterianism declines to map what it cannot solve; residualism *instruments* the residue — the PENDING graveyard and the `duhem_underdetermined` verdict register *that*, *where*, and *why* capture fails, which is the apophatic move (Pseudo-Dionysius; Nicholas of Cusa's *docta ignorantia*) made auditable rather than the defeatist one. Knowing the shape of the unknown is the opposite of surrender.

**Objection 2: Rosen's non-computability is disputed — and self-undermining.** The deepest in-principle case for R4 looks like Robert Rosen's claim (*Life Itself*, 1991; Louie, 2007) that an organism, closed to efficient causation, possesses a model that is strictly non-simulable: no Turing machine computes its modeling relation. But this is contested. Mossio, Longo, and Stewart (2009) expressed closure to efficient causation in lambda-calculus, a paradigmatic computability formalism, and Letelier, Cárdenas et al. (2010) report computable realizations of (M,R)-maps. Worse, if Rosen were right, a *computational* engine that licenses claims only when two computations agree could never license any living-cell claim at all — the thesis would saw off the branch the whole project sits on.

Reply: we concede the point and decline the premise. Residualism does **not** rest the floor on Rosen. We rest it on two arguments that need no anti-computationalism: Elsasser's *cardinality* claim (1987) that the combinatorial state-space of a single cell is "immense" (vastly exceeding the particle count of the universe), so any reductive statistics samples a measure-zero slice; and Kauffman's *non-ergodicity* (Longo, Montévil, Kauffman 2012) — the biosphere's adjacent possible is unprestatable, so the target is non-stationary. Both are explicitly consistent with physics and with computation. Rosen survives only as a *regulative* frame ("no largest model"), not as a load-bearing theorem.

**Objection 3: Isn't this just fallibilism?** Strip the machinery and you have Popper: all knowledge is conjectural, we never know we have reached the truth.

Reply: the difference is *ontological versus epistemic*, and it is sharp. Popper's floor is epistemic — the target remains full truth (ideal residue = 0); we simply never certify arrival. Residualism's floor is structural: residue is > 0 *in the limit*, for reasons of cardinality, non-ergodicity, emergence (Bedau's weak emergence, 1997; Batterman's singular-limit irreducibility, 2002), and measurement (Tal's definitional uncertainty). Note one honest debt: we cannot borrow Popper's verisimilitude to express "approach to a floor," because Miller and Tichý (1974) proved that formalism orders only true theories. The convergence engine (R3) is therefore stated in Misak's neo-Peircean idiom — beliefs that would withstand inquiry (*Truth and the End of Inquiry*, 1991) — which survives the 1974 collapse precisely because it uses no truthlikeness metric.

**Objection 4: The no-miracles realist reply.** The realist (Smart's "cosmic coincidence," 1963; Putnam's slogan, 1975; van Fraassen's "ultimate argument," 1980; Boyd, 1984) says apparent residue is just current immaturity: selective realism explains why the true parts are retained across change, predicting residue keeps shrinking toward zero.

Reply: residualism *uses* the no-miracles intuition — the agreement gate is a no-coincidence argument (Hacking; Salmon's common-cause principle; Perrin's converging determinations of Avogadro's number) — but bounds it with Laudan's pessimistic meta-induction (1981). We part from the realist on the *limit*, not the dynamics. Both hold residue shrinks; only residualism holds it shrinks to *F* > 0. And against Laudan we retain what he discards: superseded content is not pure error to forget but partly constitutive residue to instrument — Kuhn loss (Post 1971) recorded as a defeat edge, not an erasure, sometimes later regained (Midwinter & Janssen, "Kuhn Losses Regained," 2013).

### Open problems and a forward program

Three are load-bearing. (1) **The transfer premise.** The provable floors — Shannon's converse, Gödel/Tarski, Chaitin's Ω — govern formal or information-theoretic systems; transferring them to an empirical cut on a cell is analogical until the gate is modeled as a self-referential, recursively axiomatized system. We resist overclaiming here: Ω establishes *incompressible, irreducible* information, **not** positive density of undecidable sentences; a density claim needs separate work (Calude & Jürgensen on true-but-unprovable statements), not inference from one real. (2) **The Sellarsian back-door Given.** Code-level independence is not conceptual independence; agreement may track a shared inherited frame. The program is to certify and log *which* conceptual dimensions (ontology, operationalization, instrument model, statistical commitments) two implementations actually vary. (3) **Earning *F* > 0 for a specific domain**, not merely "not yet zero."

A caution on our central biological target bears restating once, in full, because the whole position depends on it: Duhem himself restricted holism to theoretical physics and *expressly excluded* physiology and the life sciences. The cell-level `duhem_underdetermined` verdict is therefore Quinean globalization — and even there, the strongest classical ally is the *later* Quine (1970; 1975), not the confirmation holism of "Two Dogmas" (1951) — and that ally half-recanted, wavering toward intertranslatability. We do not write "provably never equals the territory" on Quinean grounds (cf. Laudan & Leplin 1991).

### Naming and positioning

Against **perspectival realism** (Massimi 2022; Giere 2006): we share situated partiality (R1) but assert a residue surviving the *union* of all perspectives, and we operationalize it. Against **structural realism** (Worrall 1989; Ladyman 1998; Ladyman & Ross 2007): they prize the structure preserved across change and discard the rest as non-referential surplus; we invert the polarity and make the leftover the first-class object. Against **constructive empiricism** (van Fraassen 1980): we share the deflationary cut and the licensing-not-meaning firewall (Dewey's warranted assertibility, 1938–41), but where CE is *agnostic* about the residue, we hold it is real, structured, and informative, and we treat gate-failure as positive data.

The differentiation from neighbors is, finally, a set of contrast pairs. Peircean in *process* but anti-convergent-realist in the *limit* — and here we are careful: Peirce himself is ambivalent, not a clean zero-residue foil; his tychism, synechism, and logic of vagueness leave room for permanent residue on unposed questions. The crisp zero-residue foil is twentieth-century convergent realism (Putnam, Boyd), not Peirce. Adornian in admitting "objects do not go into their concepts without leaving a remainder," but operational where Adorno is dialectical. Two genuinely under-occupied moves remain, and they are the whole of the contribution: promoting the residue to a measured, first-class artifact, and the positive-floor commitment itself. Everything else in this paper is borrowed, with attribution; these two are what residualism adds.
