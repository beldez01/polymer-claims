# Measuring: Genomic Methods as Readout, QC, and Potency

> **Filed under:** Programmable Living Medicines > Part IX
> **Created:** 2026-06-09
> **Last updated:** 2026-06-09

## Summary

A molecule's release certificate is a chromatogram; a living drug's release certificate is a *census of cell states*. Once the therapeutic unit becomes a programmable cell — a sense→compute→act computer rather than a fixed structure — the measurement problem inverts: you can no longer fully specify the product by its composition, because two lots with identical surface phenotype and viability can differ by orders of magnitude in potency and persistence. This chapter argues that genomic and epigenomic methods are the native measurement layer of living medicine, supplying all four legs of the regulatory stool — identity, purity, potency, safety — at the resolution of single cells and single integration events. The most important and counterintuitive lesson of the field is encoded in a single 2018 patient: the entire antitumor response came from *one* CAR-T clone in which the lentivirus had landed inside *TET2*, and the only reason anyone knows this is that someone sequenced the integration site (Fraietta et al., 2018). Potency, not delivery or editing, is the true rate-limiting reagent of cell therapy, and the QC data exhaust generated while chasing it — millions of paired genotype/phenotype/outcome cells — is the most defensible commercial asset the field will produce.

## Background / First Principles

A release assay exists to discharge a single epistemic obligation: *before this product enters a patient, prove it is what we say it is, contains only what it should, does what it should, and will not do what it must not.* For small molecules this decomposes cleanly because the molecule is its own specification — purity is HPLC area-percent, identity is mass and NMR, potency is a dose-response curve on a stable target, safety is the impurity table. Each property is a low-dimensional measurement of a static object.

A programmable cell breaks every one of those assumptions:

1. **The object is high-dimensional and heterogeneous.** A CAR-T infusion is ~10⁷–10⁸ cells spanning a continuum of differentiation, exhaustion, and metabolic states. The "product" is a *distribution*, and the therapeutically relevant fraction (early-memory, T_SCM/T_CM cells) is often a minority of it (Gattinoni et al., 2011; Fraietta et al., 2018).
2. **The object is dynamic.** The cell will divide, differentiate, and rewire transcription *after* infusion, so a release measurement is a snapshot of an initial condition on a trajectory, not a final state (see [epigenetic-mitochondrial-trajectory-assay](../cell-therapy-qc/epigenetic-mitochondrial-trajectory-assay.md)).
3. **Identity is not surface-deep.** Two cells can present identical CD3/CD8/CAR flow signatures and carry opposite epigenetic fates. Surface phenotype is a lossy, late readout of a regulatory state that methylation and chromatin encode directly.
4. **The instruction set is genomic.** Because the therapeutic program is written in DNA (CAR transgene, edits, integration locus), the only assay that reads the *actual instructions installed* — including off-target edits and where the vector landed — is sequencing.

From these four facts follows the thesis of this chapter: **for a living drug, genomic/epigenomic readouts are not one option among many — they are the only measurement layer that operates at the same representational level as the medicine itself.** Surface phenotyping measures shadows; sequencing measures the source code. The information-theoretic argument is blunt: a flow panel of 8 markers resolves on the order of 2⁸ states; a single-cell transcriptome resolves a 20,000-dimensional vector. When the product's relevant variation lives in that high-dimensional space (it does), low-dimensional assays are guaranteed to alias distinct products into the same release bin.

## Key Findings

### Identity and purity: single-cell and spatial omics as the product census

Compendium release specs (sterility, endotoxin, viability, CAR⁺ fraction, CD3 purity) remain mandatory but are coarse. The frontier characterization layer is single-cell multi-omics run as **product phenotyping**:

- **scRNA-seq** resolves the differentiation/exhaustion composition of the lot. The therapeutically decisive axis is the fraction of stem-like memory (T_SCM) and central-memory (T_CM) cells versus terminally differentiated effectors; pre-infusion memory content predicts expansion and persistence (Gattinoni et al., 2011; Fraietta et al., 2018). A lot that passes viability and CAR⁺% can still be dominated by exhausted T_EX cells expressing *TOX*, *PDCD1*, *HAVCR2*, *LAG3* — invisible to a standard release panel.
- **scATAC-seq** reads the *chromatin accessibility* program, which is causally upstream of transcription and a more stable identity fingerprint. Exhaustion is enforced by a defined accessibility landscape (TOX/NR4A-driven), so scATAC distinguishes "transiently activated" from "epigenetically locked into exhaustion" — a distinction transcription alone can blur (Khan et al., 2019; Belk et al., 2022).
- **CITE-seq** (antibody-DNA barcodes read alongside mRNA) reconciles the surface phenotype that regulators and clinicians already trust with the transcriptome that actually predicts behavior, providing a translation table between the legacy flow spec and the genomic state (Stoeckius et al., 2017).
- **Spatial transcriptomics** matters most for *solid-tumor* and tissue-resident products and for autopsy/biopsy pharmacodynamics: where did the infused cells go, and what state did the microenvironment drive them into?

The counterintuitive operational point: these assays are presently positioned as *characterization* (development-phase, not lot-release) because they are destructive, slow, and not yet harmonized. But they are the only methods that measure the product at the resolution at which it varies. The field's QC architecture is mid-migration from surface-phenotype release toward genomic-state release, gated by cost, turnaround, and inter-site reproducibility rather than by scientific validity.

### Lineage tracing, clonal barcoding, and integration-site analysis: the *in vivo* fate of the program

Once cells are infused, the question becomes which clones expanded, persisted, or transformed — a genomics-only question. Three tools answer it:

- **Integration-site (IS) analysis.** For integrating vectors (lentivirus, γ-retrovirus), the vector–genome junction is a unique, heritable barcode per transduced founder clone. Sequencing IS distributions (e.g., LAM-PCR, or tagmentation-based methods recovering 1,034–6,233 sites per sample; Kim et al., 2023) reconstructs clonal architecture and flags oligoclonal or monoclonal outgrowth — the cardinal signal of insertional mutagenesis. This is simultaneously a **safety** assay (is one clone taking over because the vector hit an oncogene?) and a **mechanism-of-potency** assay (which founder won, and why?).
- **The Fraietta lesson.** In a CLL patient with a durable CD19 CAR-T response, ~94% of CAR-T cells at peak derived from a *single* clone in which the CAR transgene had inserted into and disrupted *TET2*, with a hypomorphic second allele; *TET2* loss drove a central-memory epigenetic program with enhanced proliferation and cytokine output (Fraietta et al., 2018). The therapeutic win was discovered only because the integration site was sequenced. This is the canonical demonstration that **the genomic readout is not bookkeeping — it can be the only way to learn how the drug works.** It also seeds the inverse, deliberate-engineering program (TET2 knockdown to manufacture better CAR-T) discussed in [Part V](./05-writing-epigenome-cell-state.md) and the genotype-directed effector logic of [genotype-directed-cytotoxicity](../opto-car/seed-report.md).
- **The CBL counterexample and the safety frontier.** The same biology is double-edged: a documented anti-CD22 CAR-T case showed a clone with vector integration in *CBL* (a tumor suppressor) transiently expanding to ~half of all white cells (Shah et al., 2019). Insertional mutagenesis is not hypothetical — in January 2024 the FDA mandated boxed warnings for T-cell malignancy across all approved BCMA- and CD19-directed autologous CAR-T products after ≥22 reported T-cell cancers, some carrying the CAR transgene within the malignant clone (FDA, 2024). IS analysis is the assay that distinguishes benign vector landing from emerging insertional oncogenesis, though most secondary malignancies are now attributed more to immune dysfunction and pre-existing clonal hematopoiesis than to insertion itself (Levine et al., 2024).

Synthetic **DNA/RNA barcode libraries** (random N-mers transduced before infusion) generalize IS tracing to non-integrating and edited products, giving each founder a sequenceable serial number for clonal fate-mapping without relying on the vector junction.

### Methylation as a stable identity and potency biomarker; clocks as product "age"

DNA methylation is the most attractive QC substrate for a living drug for a first-principles reason: it is **digital, covalent, mitotically heritable, and read by an established sequencing/array stack**, so it captures cell *state and history* in a way that transient RNA or labile surface markers cannot. Three uses:

1. **Identity fingerprint.** Cell type is more faithfully defined by its methylome than by any marker panel; lineage and even clonal history leave stable methylation marks (Farlik et al., 2016). This is the same logic the [CHIP methylation-biology](../chip/02_methylation_biology.md) and [epigenetics](../epigenetics/methylation-gene-regulation.md) reports develop for hematopoiesis.
2. **Manufacturing scar / potency biomarker.** CAR-T culture imprints a reproducible, drift-like DNA hypermethylation at function-relevant loci (*TCF7*, *LEF1*, *FOXO3*, exhaustion-linked programs), and methylation at culture-associated CpGs in clinical products associates with post-infusion survival — a candidate QC biomarker that surface phenotype does not capture (Salz et al., 2023; the headline loci are *TCF7*, *LEF1*, *RUNX1*, and *TOX*, with *FOXO3* discussed secondarily).
3. **Epigenetic clocks as "product age"/exhaustion.** Methylation clocks (Horvath, 2013; Hannum et al., 2013) and their immune-specific successors quantify a biological age decoupled from manufacturing-day count. The compelling QC reframing — developed in [epigenetic-clocks](../chip/03_epigenetic_clocks.md) — is that a *replication/exhaustion clock* could read how much "differentiation runway" remains in a lot: a chronologically young culture that is epigenetically "old" has spent its expansion potential and will likely fail to persist. The clock measures the state variable (remaining naïve/stem-like capacity) that day-count and viability only proxy.

The honest caveat (carried from the trajectory-assay report): culture-associated methylation associations may partly capture patient biology, starting-material quality, or culture time rather than a causal potency lever, and 5hmC/5mC confounding plagues TET-pathway loci specifically. The defensible claim is *added predictive information beyond day-count, phenotype, viability, and CAR fraction* — not standalone causation.

### MRD by sequencing: the efficacy readout for clone-elimination therapies

For any living medicine whose job is to *eliminate a defined clone* — CD19/BCMA CAR-T, and prospectively the genotype-directed killers of [genotype-directed-cytotoxicity](../opto-car/seed-report.md) — the natural efficacy endpoint is the abundance of the target clone, measured by sequencing. **clonoSEQ** (Adaptive Biotechnologies) is the FDA-cleared NGS assay that identifies a patient-specific clonotypic immunoglobulin/TCR rearrangement at diagnosis and then tracks it to a sensitivity of one malignant cell in ~10⁶ healthy cells (10⁻⁶) in B-ALL, multiple myeloma, and CLL (Adaptive Biotechnologies; FDA clearances). MRD-negativity at 10⁻⁶ is increasingly a surrogate for durable response and a deepening regulatory endpoint.

The first-principles point for *this* treatise: a clone-elimination therapy and a clone-counting assay are two halves of one control loop. The therapy *acts* on clone abundance; MRD-seq *measures* the controlled variable. This closes the sense→compute→act loop at the patient-system level — the readout is the error signal. For genotype-gated effectors that target a *driver variant* (TET2, JAK2-V617F, DNMT3A) rather than a clonal rearrangement, the corresponding MRD readout is error-corrected duplex/UMI sequencing of the variant VAF — the same measurement layer that defines clonal hematopoiesis ([CHIP detection-assays](../chip/06_detection_assays.md)).

### The "universal potency" problem and why it is the field's true bottleneck

Potency is where the QC stack is weakest and where the most value is trapped. The regulatory definition — a quantitative measure of the *specific ability to effect a given result* — demands a functional readout, but the field's manufacturing landscape has fragmented into at least seven concurrent paradigms (fast autologous, expansion-light, 24-hour, point-of-care, allogeneic ex vivo, iPSC-derived, and *in vivo* CAR-T), each with a different potency question and, critically, *in vivo* CAR-T offering no manufactured cell to test at all ([universal-functional-potency-2026](../cell-therapy-qc/universal-functional-potency-2026.md)). The single question surviving every paradigm is: *do these cells kill, and are they fit?*

Genomics partly answers this. A **molecular potency signature** — a transcriptomic/epigenomic state predictive of cytotoxic function and persistence — can serve as a surrogate potency assay where a direct killing assay is impractical (e.g., reading PD on *in-vivo*-generated CAR-T from patient blood). The FDA's 2023 Potency Assurance framework and its January 11, 2026 CMC-flexibility posture explicitly invite matrix/alternative potency methods if mechanistically justified and clinically bridged (FDA, 2023; FDA, 2026), which is the regulatory opening for genomic surrogate-potency assays. But the load-bearing caveat is **Goodhart's law**: a transcriptomic signature trained on outcome is a proxy, and optimizing manufacturing against the proxy can decouple it from the function it was meant to predict. Genomic potency surrogates must be periodically re-anchored to a *direct* functional readout (killing, OXPHOS fitness; Goldberg et al., 2026), which is precisely the gap single-cell functional platforms aim to fill.

## Counterintuitive & Groundbreaking Applications

**1. QC data exhaust is the database moat.** Every lot released generates paired data — installed program (sequenced edits/IS), product state (sc-multiome/methylome), and eventually clinical outcome (MRD trajectory, persistence, response). Individually these are compliance artifacts; *aggregated and linked*, they form a genotype/epigenotype→phenotype→outcome map that no single experiment can buy. The first organization to accumulate millions of such linked cells owns the function that converts "release spec" into "response prediction." The defensible asset of cell-therapy manufacturing may turn out to be not the construct or the process but the **labeled QC corpus** — a direct analogue to the [machine-readable-biology](../precision-medicine/machine-readable-biology.md) thesis and to the data-moat logic running through the CytoWell potency strategy.

**2. Potency, not delivery or editing, is the rate-limiting reagent.** The treatise's earlier parts solve reading, writing, and computing. This chapter's contrarian claim is that *measuring whether the resulting cell works* is harder and less solved than building it. Delivery and editing have quantitative, improving yardsticks; potency has a definitional crisis (seven paradigms, no shared assay, *in vivo* with no testable cell). Capital follows the bottleneck: the >$2B *in vivo* CAR-T pharma deals of 2025–2026 ([universal-functional-potency-2026](../cell-therapy-qc/universal-functional-potency-2026.md)) bought modalities whose release-grade potency assay *does not yet exist*. The whitespace is the assay, not the asset.

**3. The clock as a release gate, not a research curiosity.** Reframing an epigenetic exhaustion clock as a *remaining-runway* spec — reject lots that are epigenetically "spent" regardless of viability — would be the first prospective use of an aging biomarker as a manufacturing release criterion. It inverts the clock's usual diagnostic framing into a forward-looking potency predictor.

**4. Integration-site analysis as built-in mechanism discovery.** The Fraietta case implies that for integrating products, *routine* IS sequencing is not merely safety surveillance — it is a continuous, population-scale screen for fortuitous potency-enhancing insertions. The manufacturing line becomes an unbiased forward-genetic screen for "which genomic context makes a better cell," with the clinic as the selection.

**5. MRD-seq closes the therapeutic control loop.** Coupling a genotype-directed effector to error-corrected variant MRD turns one-shot dosing into closed-loop, measurement-guided therapy: dose, measure residual clone, re-dose or escalate logic. This is the patient-scale realization of the sense→compute→act architecture, with sequencing as the feedback sensor.

## Open Questions

- **Can a genomic/epigenomic signature ever *replace* a functional potency assay, or only augment it?** The Goodhart risk says augment-and-re-anchor; the practical pressure (in vivo CAR-T) pushes toward replace. Where the line settles is unresolved.
- **What is the right reference state for an "exhaustion clock"?** Clocks are trained on chronological aging; T-cell exhaustion is a distinct (if overlapping) program. A purpose-built replicative/exhaustion clock for cell products does not yet exist as a validated release tool.
- **How much of culture-associated methylation drift is causal versus a passenger of patient/starting-material variation?** Until disentangled, the methylation biomarker is correlative.
- **Can single-cell multi-omics be made fast, cheap, non-destructive, and harmonized enough for lot release** rather than characterization? Turnaround and inter-site reproducibility, not validity, are the blockers.
- **For allogeneic and iPSC-derived products, what is the identity/clonality spec** when the "clone" is a manufactured line rather than a patient founder? IS and barcode logic must be redefined.
- **Does the boxed-warning T-cell malignancy risk reflect insertion or pre-existing CHIP?** The answer determines whether IS analysis or pre-manufacture CHIP screening is the dominant safety assay (Levine et al., 2024).
