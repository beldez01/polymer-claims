# Computing: Synthetic Gene Circuits and Cellular Logic

## Summary

Between the **sense** layer (Part V) and the **act** layer (Part VII) sits the part of a living therapeutic that is most genuinely a *computer*: the circuit that takes molecular inputs and returns a decision. This chapter argues that the therapeutic window — the gap between killing tumor and sparing self — is not a chemistry problem but a **classification problem**, and that the discipline of building reliable classifiers out of biomolecules is governed by hard, quantitative limits: gene-expression is stochastic (Poisson-floor noise that scales as 1/√N in copy number), every added node loads the upstream node (retroactivity), every gate leaks (finite ON/OFF ratio), and cascaded gates multiply their error rates so that deep logic *degrades* rather than sharpens. We survey the realized computing primitives — synNotch transcriptional AND gates (Roybal/Lim 2016), recombinase state machines that write irreversible memory into DNA (Lu lab, Science 2016), RNA-level logic (toehold switches with >400-fold dynamic range; ADAR AND/OR sensors), and analog feedback controllers that buy robustness through integral action (antithetic/Khammash control) — and map each onto the clinical logic gates already in patients: A2 Bio's Tmod **NOT** gate keyed to HLA-A\*02 loss of heterozygosity, and Senti's **CD33 OR FLT3 NOT EMCN** CAR-NK for AML. The counterintuitive thesis is that **shallow, analog, feedback-stabilized logic beats deep digital cascades**, that the most defensible therapeutic IP is the *circuit topology* rather than the binder, and that genomic loss events (LOH, allele dropout) are the richest, most under-exploited input variable in the entire design space.

## Background / First Principles

A therapeutic cell must answer a question — *is this cell I am touching one I should kill?* — using only the molecular information available at the synapse. Formally this is **binary classification under noise**, and the relevant first principles are information-theoretic and control-theoretic, not pharmacological.

**1. The unit of computation is a molecular concentration, and concentrations are noisy.** Gene expression is bursty and stochastic. For a species present at mean copy number *N*, the Fano-limited coefficient of variation cannot fall below ~1/√N; at the tens-to-hundreds of molecules typical of a transcription factor or a synthetic receptor's downstream reporter, intrinsic noise of 20–50% CV is the *floor*, before extrinsic cell-to-cell variation (Elowitz et al., *Science* 2002; Raj & van Oudenaarden, *Cell* 2008). A logic gate that must separate "ON" from "OFF" is therefore separating two overlapping distributions, and the achievable false-positive/false-negative rate is set by the distance between distribution means relative to their spread — a signal-to-noise problem identical to a radio receiver's.

**2. Digital abstraction is a *lie the circuit tells*, and the lie costs energy and fidelity.** Electronic logic restores signal at every gate (a transistor pins its output to rail voltages), so a 64-gate cascade is as crisp as one gate. Biology has no free restoration. Each genetic gate has a finite **dynamic range** (ON/OFF ratio): a synthetic transcriptional gate might achieve 10–100×, a well-engineered toehold switch >400× (Green et al., *Cell* 2014). When you wire gate B downstream of gate A, B's OFF state is contaminated by A's leak and B's ON state is capped by A's incomplete activation. Error compounds **multiplicatively** down a cascade. This is the central reason deep biological logic is fragile and shallow logic is robust — a point we return to as the chapter's main engineering thesis.

**3. Composition is not free: retroactivity and resource load.** Connecting a downstream module to an upstream one changes the upstream module's behavior, because the downstream binding sites sequester upstream signal — **retroactivity**, the biomolecular analog of impedance/fan-out loading (Del Vecchio, Ninfa & Sontag, *Mol Syst Biol* 2008). Separately, all synthetic genes draw on shared ribosomes, RNA polymerases, and amino acids; expressing one circuit gene throttles the others (**resource competition**, a hidden coupling that makes nominally independent gates interact; Gyorgy et al., *Biophys J* 2015; Qian et al. 2017). Both effects mean a circuit is never the clean diagram on the whiteboard.

**4. Robustness must be *engineered in*, and integral feedback is the unique solution to perfect adaptation.** A foundational result of control theory, imported into synthetic biology by Khammash and colleagues, is that **robust perfect adaptation** — holding an output at setpoint despite arbitrary, persistent disturbances and parameter variation — *requires* integral feedback, and the minimal biomolecular realization is the **antithetic integral feedback** motif: two controller species that annihilate each other, so their difference integrates the error (Briat, Gupta & Khammash, *Cell Systems* 2016). A mammalian realization of this class of controller — a proportional–integral feedback circuit for robust, precise gene regulation — has now been built (Frei et al., *PNAS* 2022; step-by-step antithetic-integral build in Frei & Khammash, *Methods Mol Biol* 2024). The lesson for therapeutics: a circuit that must hold a precise output (a controlled secretion rate, a bounded proliferation) in the wildly variable in-vivo environment cannot rely on open-loop tuning; it needs closed-loop integral control.

**5. The therapeutic window IS a computation.** A monospecific CAR computes a one-bit function: `kill = (antigen > threshold)`. Its toxicity is the integral of off-tumor antigen expression. Every multi-input logic gate is an attempt to *raise the effective specificity* by computing a richer Boolean function of several inputs whose joint distribution separates tumor from self better than any single input. The window is literally the margin of a classifier. This reframing — that we are building cellular classifiers and the clinic is their test set — organizes the rest of the chapter.

## Key Findings

### Digital primitives I — transcriptional logic via synNotch (AND)

The **synNotch** receptor (Morsut et al., *Cell* 2016; Roybal et al., *Cell* 2016) is the workhorse of cellular digital logic. It hijacks the Notch core: ligand binding to a custom extracellular scFv induces intramembrane proteolysis (ADAM/γ-secretase) that releases a custom intracellular transcription factor (e.g., Gal4-VP64), which then drives any cargo — most usefully, *a CAR against a second antigen*. This implements a two-input **AND gate** ("prime-and-kill"): antigen A licenses the cell to make a killer against antigen B, so cytotoxicity requires A AND B in cis/local proximity (Roybal et al., *Cell* 2016, "Precision Tumor Recognition by T Cells With Combinatorial Antigen-Sensing Circuits").

The clinically motivating instance is glioblastoma: EGFRvIII is tumor-specific but heterogeneously expressed, while EphA2/IL13Rα2 are homogeneous but also on normal brain. A synNotch-EGFRvIII → CAR-EphA2/IL13Rα2 circuit primes only in the tumor and kills broadly within it, decoupling the specificity antigen from the killing antigen (Choe et al., *Sci Transl Med* 2021). This is the canonical demonstration that **logic widens the window**: neither antigen alone is safe, but their AND is.

First-principles caveats on synNotch as a digital gate: it is **slow** (proteolysis + transcription + translation; hours, not seconds), **leaky** (basal proteolysis gives target-independent CAR expression), and **history-dependent** (once primed, the cell stays armed for a while — a soft memory that can cause bystander killing after the cell leaves antigen A). Its ON/OFF is good but finite, which bounds how many synNotch layers can be stacked.

### Digital primitives II — split receptors, dimerization, and dCas logic

Beyond synNotch, the digital toolkit includes:

- **Split/conditional receptors**: the CAR's signaling is divided across two chains, each binding a different antigen, so signaling reconstitutes only on co-engagement (AND), or a co-stimulation-only chain tunes a weak signal-1 chain. ON-switch CARs gate assembly on a small-molecule dimerizer (rapamycin/AP21967), making killing drug-AND-antigen (Wu et al., *Science* 2015).
- **dCas9/dCas12 transcriptional logic**: catalytically dead Cas fused to activators (VPR) or repressors (KRAB) builds programmable NOR/NAND gates at the promoter level, with the guide-RNA sequence as the rewireable wire. Layered dCas circuits implement multi-input Boolean functions (Gander et al., *Nat Commun* 2017; Kim et al. CRISPR logic). The appeal is *programmability of the wiring* by changing 20-nt guides; the cost is the retroactivity and resource-load of many guides sharing one dCas pool.

All of these are **combinational** logic: output is a function of present inputs only. The next primitive adds *time*.

### Digital primitives III — recombinase state machines (memory and sequence logic)

Combinational gates forget. To compute on the *order* of events — was the cell exposed to A before B? — you need **sequential logic with state**, and biology's cleanest implementation is the **recombinase state machine**. Serine integrases (Bxb1, phiC31, etc.) flip or excise DNA segments flanked by attB/attP sites; the reaction is **irreversible** without a directionality factor, so each recombination **writes a permanent bit into the genome**. Roquet, Lu and colleagues built integrase state machines that record the **temporal order** of multiple chemical inputs by routing through distinct DNA configurations — recording all five orderings of two inputs and driving state-dependent gene programs (Roquet et al., *Science* 2016, "Synthetic recombinase-based state machines in living cells"). Because the bit is in DNA, the memory is heritable and analog-noise-immune (you cannot lose a flipped segment to expression noise).

Therapeutic implications are large and underused. A recombinase state machine could record a *differentiation history* or an *exposure sequence* (saw bone-marrow niche → then saw tumor antigen) and only then commit to a killing program — sequence logic that no combinational CAR can express. It is also the natural substrate for **single-use safety latches**: an integrase that, once tripped by a danger signal, permanently excises the CAR or arms a suicide gene. The cost: integration is **one-shot and slow**, and off-target integrase activity is a genotoxicity concern (insertional mutagenesis), so the writing must be tightly gated.

### Digital primitives IV — RNA-level logic (toehold switches, ADAR AND/OR)

Protein logic is slow and resource-heavy. **RNA logic** is faster, cheaper, and sequence-programmable. **Toehold switches** (Green et al., *Cell* 2014) are de-novo riboregulators: a hairpin sequesters the ribosome-binding site until a trigger RNA invades via an exposed toehold, opening the hairpin to permit translation — achieving average ON/OFF ratios **>400**, a digital crispness usually reserved for protein regulators, with orthogonal libraries enabling multi-input AND from concatenated triggers. Translation-*repressing* riboregulators (Kim et al., *Nat Chem Biol* 2019) complete the NOT/NAND set, enabling de-novo multi-input cellular logic in RNA.

The therapeutically decisive RNA primitive is **ADAR-based sensing** (CellREADR/RADARS/RADAR; Qian et al., *Nature* 2022; Kaseniit et al. and Jiang et al., *Nat Biotechnol* 2023), covered in depth in [genotype-directed-cytotoxicity.md](../opto-car/seed-report.md). The relevant point *for this chapter* is that ADAR sensors are not merely sensors — **they are logic gates**. A sensor whose STOP-readthrough requires a target transcript implements an implicit AND with the cell's translation machinery; concatenated sensors implement **AND across two transcripts**; parallel sensors driving a common payload implement **OR**; and, critically, the sensor can discriminate a **single base**, making the *input wire itself a genotype classifier* rather than a protein-abundance threshold. RNA logic thus lets the "compute" layer read the genome directly — the deepest move available, because it gets beneath surface phenotype to the driver mutation.

### Analog computation and feedback control

Not all useful cellular computation is Boolean. Much of physiology is **analog**: graded responses, ratiometric sensing, proportional dosing. Analog circuits compute more per part — a single transcriptional unit can perform addition, ratio, and power-law operations in log-concentration space (Daniel et al., *Nature* 2013) — and they sidestep the noise penalty of forcing a graded signal into a hard threshold. The clinical relevance: a secretion-circuit that should release IL-12 *proportionally* to tumor burden (and shut off as burden falls) is an analog controller, not a switch.

The deepest analog tool is **feedback control for robustness**. As above, **antithetic integral feedback** (Briat, Gupta & Khammash, *Cell Systems* 2016) is the minimal biomolecular motif that guarantees robust perfect adaptation — output returns exactly to setpoint despite disturbances — and the controller class has been realized in mammalian cells (Frei et al., *PNAS* 2022). For a living drug, integral feedback is how you make a circuit's output **insensitive to the patient**: cell-to-cell variation in copy number, the inflamed-vs-quiescent microenvironment, and immune pressure all become disturbances the controller rejects. Proportional-integral (PI) variants trade some adaptation precision for reduced variance and faster settling (Aoki et al., *Nature* 2019; PNAS 2022). The under-appreciated message: **the robustness of a cell therapy is a control-theory property you can design for, not a stroke of luck you hope for.**

### The reliability ceiling — why deep cascades degrade

Synthesize the first principles into the chapter's load-bearing engineering claim. Let a single genetic gate have ON/OFF dynamic range *D* and per-gate error probability *ε* (a leak event reading as ON, or a dropout reading as OFF). For combinational depth *k* in series, the effective signal contrast falls roughly as the product of the per-stage contrasts and the error accumulates super-linearly; with realistic *D* ~ 10–100 and *ε* of a few percent, **3–4 layers is the practical ceiling** before the OFF leak floods the output and the classifier's margin collapses. This is why every *clinically* advanced logic-gated cell uses **two, at most three, inputs** — never the elaborate 6-input genetic computers that look impressive in a bacterial reporter assay. Noise (1/√N), retroactivity (loading), resource competition (shared ribosomes), and finite *D* (leak) are four independent taxes, and they all worsen with depth. The corollary is the chapter's design rule: **buy specificity with better inputs and feedback stabilization, not with more gates.** A two-input AND on two well-chosen, well-separated antigens beats a five-input genetic epic on noisy ones.

## Counterintuitive & Groundbreaking Applications

### The therapeutic window is a classifier margin — and LOH is the best free input

Reframing the window as classification exposes which inputs are valuable: not the most tumor-enriched antigen, but the input with the **largest, most reliable separation between tumor and self distributions**. The most elegant such input is a **genomic loss event** rather than a gain. A2 Bio's **Tmod** platform builds a **NOT gate** whose blocker is keyed to the *germline* HLA-A\*02 allele that the tumor has **lost via loss of heterozygosity (LOH)**: an activator targets a broadly expressed antigen (CEA, mesothelin, EGFR) while an inhibitory receptor senses HLA-A\*02 and *vetoes* killing of any cell that still displays it. Normal cells keep both copies and are protected; tumor cells that have undergone HLA-A\*02 LOH lose the veto and are killed (`kill = activator AND NOT HLA-A*02`). This is in patients: **A2B530** (CEA, EVEREST-1, NCT05736731, first dosed May 2023), **A2B694** (mesothelin), and **A2B395** (EGFR, allogeneic). The counterintuitive beauty is that LOH is a **somatic genomic deletion present in ~10–20% of many solid tumors**, irreversible, and *self-revealing as an absence* — the tumor cannot re-express what it deleted to escape the NOT gate, unlike antigen-loss escape from an activating CAR. **Genomic loss is a more escape-resistant input than antigen gain**, and it is essentially free information sitting in every standard tumor sequencing report (Hwang et al., *npj Precision Oncology* 2024, showing HLA-LOH detectable in routine diagnostic NGS).

### OR-gates for heterogeneity, NOT-gates for the reservoir: the AML proof

Senti Biosciences' **SENTI-202** is the clinical demonstration that logic widens the window *in blood cancers*, where the spare-the-stem-cell problem is acute. It is an off-the-shelf CAR-NK computing **(CD33 OR FLT3) AND NOT EMCN**: an OR-gate of two activating CARs covers AML's antigen heterogeneity (blasts and leukemic stem cells express CD33 and/or FLT3, rarely uniformly), while an **Endomucin (EMCN) inhibitory CAR** is the NOT-gate that protects healthy hematopoietic stem/progenitor cells — solving exactly the "you cannot ablate the patient's marrow" caveat that haunts genotype-directed clone killing (see §7 of [genotype-directed-cytotoxicity.md](../opto-car/seed-report.md)). Senti reported positive preliminary Phase 1 data (AACR 2025). The general principle: **OR buys coverage of a heterogeneous target; NOT carves out the protected reservoir.** AML — where the malignant and normal compartments share most surface antigens and differ mainly in combinatorial pattern — is the purest possible argument that single-antigen targeting is under-powered and that the window is recoverable only by computing a richer Boolean function.

### Time-logic as a safety primitive, and circuits as IP

Recombinase state machines open a frontier the field has barely touched: **sequence- and history-gated therapy**. A cell that kills only if it encountered a tissue-localizing signal *before* a tumor antigen (correct anatomical order) would be far safer than one that kills on co-incidence anywhere. A one-shot integrase safety latch that *permanently* commits a runaway cell to apoptosis is an irreversibility that protein switches cannot match. And antithetic integral feedback turns "the cell secretes a controlled, bounded amount of a toxic cytokine and *holds* that setpoint regardless of patient variability" from aspiration into a designable specification.

Commercially, the chapter's sharpest claim: **the defensible asset is the circuit topology, not the binder.** Binders (scFvs, nanobodies) are increasingly commoditized and designable; the *logic that combines them* — the specific NOT-gate keyed to LOH (A2 Bio's Tmod estate), the OR/NOT triplet for AML (Senti), the synNotch prime-and-kill architecture (Cell Design Labs → Gilead) — is what is patented and licensed. A circuit is a **reusable classifier chassis**: the same Tmod NOT-gate ports across CEA, MSLN, and EGFR activators by swapping one binder. This is the cell-therapy analog of owning the instruction-set rather than the program — and it is why "circuits as IP" will dominate the next decade of cell-therapy dealmaking. (This connects directly to the IP analysis in §12 of [genotype-directed-cytotoxicity.md](../opto-car/seed-report.md): nobody owns the *composition*, and the composition is the circuit.)

### The most provocative move: read the genome, not the proteome, as the input wire

The boldest synthesis is to make the *input* to the logic an ADAR/Cas13 RNA sensor that classifies a **single driver base** (JAK2 V617F, IDH R132, KRAS G12) and feed that bit into a conventional logic-gated effector. This collapses the sense and compute layers — the sensor *is* the first gate — and gives a classifier whose discriminating variable is the mutation that *defines* the malignancy and that the cell cannot drop without surrendering its fitness (the driver-dependence moat). The reliability ceiling argument warns against stacking many such sensors; the right design is a **shallow AND** of one genotype sensor and one tissue/safety NOT-gate, feedback-stabilized — exactly the two-input, integral-controlled architecture the first-principles analysis predicts is the sweet spot.

## Open Questions

- **What is the real depth ceiling in primary human cells in vivo?** Most multi-input genetic logic is characterized in cell lines or bacteria. The *in-vivo* error rates of synNotch/ADAR/dCas gates under immune pressure, exhaustion, and antigen modulation are largely unmeasured — and they set the true classifier margin.
- **Can integral feedback survive the cell-therapy context?** Antithetic control is proven in cultured mammalian cells; whether it holds setpoint across in-vivo expansion, contraction, and differentiation of a T/NK product — where the controller genes themselves are subject to silencing and copy-number drift — is unknown.
- **Retroactivity and resource load at therapeutic scale.** As circuits add gates (and clinical products increasingly stack 3–4 transgenes), shared-resource coupling and loading may produce emergent failure modes (oscillation, bistability, output collapse) not seen in two-gene demos. Insulation devices (load drivers, sRNA-based decoupling) exist in principle; none is validated in a clinical cell.
- **Leak versus the rare cell.** A 1% gate leak is invisible in a bulk assay but lethal if it licenses killing of even a small fraction of an essential normal population (e.g., HSCs). The acceptable leak for a NOT-gate protecting the marrow reservoir may be orders of magnitude below what current gates deliver.
- **Memory's double edge.** Recombinase and synNotch "memory" make cells safer (commitment, latches) and more dangerous (armed cells that wander out of the licensing context). The kinetics of disarming — how fast a primed cell forgets — are poorly characterized and clinically consequential.
- **Analog vs. digital for dosing.** Is graded analog control of a secreted payload safer (smooth, proportional) or more dangerous (no hard off) than a digital switch? The control-theoretic answer depends on the payload's therapeutic index and the loop's stability margins — neither is established for in-vivo cytokine circuits.
