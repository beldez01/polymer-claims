# Acting: Cellular Effectors and Synthetic Immunology

## Summary

This chapter covers the **act** layer of the programmable cell: once a synthetic circuit has *sensed* a genotype/microenvironment and *computed* a decision (Parts III–VI), some chassis must execute it — by killing, secreting, suppressing, or differentiating. I argue from first principles that the effector is best understood as a **generic actuator decoupled from its targeting logic**, and that the field is converging — through universal/adapter CARs and through in-vivo cell generation — on exactly that decoupling. The dominant cytotoxic mechanism is mechanical and enzymatic (immune-synapse delivery of perforin/granzyme), it is **serial** (a single CAR-T can kill 2–3 targets in succession; ~22% do so [Davenport et al., 2015]), and it is fundamentally **rate- and dose-limited by exhaustion and tonic signaling** rather than by per-kill potency. The chassis menu — αβ-T (CAR/TCR), NK (off-the-shelf, e.g. SENTI-202), macrophage (CAR-M, e.g. CT-0508), Treg (the *inverse* of killing, e.g. QEL-001/TX200), and HSC — each trades cytotoxic power against persistence, manufacturability, immunogenicity, and the ability to act where T cells cannot reach. The two most consequential moves of 2024–2026 are (1) **adapter CARs** that rent one "kill engine" to any sensor via a switchable bridge, and (2) **in-vivo CAR generation** (LNP-mRNA: Capstan/AbbVie CPTX2309; lentivirus: Umoja VivoVec, Interius INT2104, EsoBiotec/AstraZeneca ESO-T01) that collapses the autologous manufacturing bottleneck and COGS. Together these turn the effector into a commodity rented by the sensor — the commercial inversion this Part is named for.

## Background / First Principles

**What "acting" must physically accomplish.** A therapeutic decision is information; killing is thermodynamics. To destroy a target cell the effector must irreversibly perturb it — punch its membrane, trip its death program, or deny it a survival signal. Cytotoxic lymphocytes solve this with a directional secretory weapon. Upon receptor engagement, the T/NK cell forms an **immune synapse**: a micron-scale adhesive contact (a peripheral LFA-1/ICAM-1 ring, a central signaling zone) across a cleft of ~15 nm, into which the microtubule-organizing center delivers **lytic granules** containing **perforin** (a pore-former) and **granzymes** (apoptosis-inducing serine proteases). Perforin oligomerizes in the target membrane to form Ca²⁺-dependent pores that admit granzyme B, which cleaves caspases and BID to drive apoptosis (reviewed in Dustin & Long, and the granule-exocytosis literature). This is the **perforin/granzyme axis** — fast, contact-dependent, and the workhorse of CAR cytotoxicity. The parallel **death-receptor axis** (FasL→Fas, TRAIL→DR4/5) is slower, requires the target to express an intact extrinsic apoptosis machinery, and matters more for fratricide, bystander killing, and resistance escape than for the primary hit.

**Why the synapse geometry matters for synthetic design.** CAR synapses are *not* TCR synapses. Davenport et al. (PNAS, 2018) showed CAR-T cells form a **disorganized, non-classical synapse** — Lck recruited in a diffuse pattern, lytic granules recruited *faster*, and target detachment *faster* — than a TCR synapse against the same antigen. The functional consequence: CARs deliver a lethal hit and disengage quickly, enabling **serial killing**. The same group's time-lapse single-cell work quantified this: approximately 22% of CAR-T cells sequentially delivered a lethal hit to multiple targets, at a serial-killing frequency comparable to TCR-mediated killing over the first ~20 h (Davenport et al., *Cancer Immunol Res*, 2015). The first-principles lesson: a CAR's value is not only its affinity but its **off-rate from a dying cell** — too sticky and the killer is monogamous; too loose and it never delivers a hit. This is the synaptic analog of the catalytic turnover problem (one enzyme, many substrates), and it is why affinity-detuned CARs sometimes *out-kill* high-affinity ones.

**The effector as actuator, sensing as a separable module.** A conventional CAR fuses sensing (the scFv ectodomain) and acting (the CD3ζ/costimulatory endodomain) into one polypeptide. But these are logically independent: the kill machinery does not care *why* it was triggered. Decoupling them — letting any sensor drive a generic effector — is the unifying theme of synthetic immunology and the synthesis point with Part III's *induced surface tags* and the RNA-sensing actuators of [genotype-directed cytotoxicity](../opto-car/seed-report.md). That report builds the **sense→tag** half (an ADAR/Cas13 variant sensor painting a synthetic surface flag); this chapter builds the **tag→kill** half (the universal effector that reads the flag). They are two ends of the same two-component machine.

## Key Findings

### 1. The effector chassis menu — a quantitative trade space

| Chassis | Cytotoxic mechanism | Persistence | Allo / off-the-shelf? | Reaches solid tumor? | Lead clinical exemplar |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **αβ CAR-T** | Perforin/granzyme + FasL; serial | Months–years (memory) | No (GVHD, rejection) without editing | Poorly (trafficking, TME) | CD19/BCMA approvals |
| **CAR-NK** | Perforin/granzyme + ADCC + innate receptors | Weeks (short) | **Yes** — no GVHD | Poorly | SENTI-202 (AML), CD19 cord-blood NK |
| **CAR-M (macrophage)** | Phagocytosis + antigen presentation + TME remodeling | Short, non-dividing | Partial (iPSC-derived) | **Yes — infiltrates** | CT-0508 (HER2) |
| **CAR-Treg** | *Suppression*, not killing | Long (with FOXP3 lock) | No (today) | n/a — tolerance | QEL-001, TX200 |
| **HSC / iPSC** | Indirect (reconstitution, factory) | Lifelong | iPSC = off-the-shelf | n/a | preclinical/early |

**αβ T cells** are the cytotoxic gold standard: serial killers with memory persistence, but autologous manufacturing is slow, costly (~$300k–$500k COGS-adjacent list prices), and the cells must traffic into hostile solid-tumor microenvironments where they exhaust.

**NK cells** are the off-the-shelf answer. They kill via perforin/granzyme *and* antibody-dependent cytotoxicity (CD16) *and* missing-self recognition, and crucially they **do not cause GVHD**, so they can be banked from healthy donors. Liu et al. (NEJM, 2020) treated 11 patients with cord-blood–derived, IL-15–armored, suicide-gene–equipped CD19 CAR-NK cells: **73% (8/11) response**, 7 complete responses, **no CRS, no neurotoxicity, no GVHD**, with persistence to one year. The cost is short persistence and weaker per-cell potency. **Senti Bio's SENTI-202** (off-the-shelf, iPSC-adjacent CAR-NK for AML) is the logic-gated frontier: a **CD33 OR FLT3** activating gate AND a **NOT-EMCN** inhibitory gate to spare healthy endothelium/marrow — 2/3 patients at the lowest dose (1×10⁹ CAR⁺ NK) reached complete remission as of the Sept-2024 cutoff (Senti Bio, 2024–2025). This is Part V's logic made flesh in an off-the-shelf effector.

**Macrophages (CAR-M)** invert the trade space: they are professional phagocytes that **infiltrate solid tumors** (the one thing T cells fail at), remodel an immunosuppressive TME toward M1, and present antigen to recruit endogenous T cells — a built-in epitope-spreading amplifier. Carisma's **CT-0508** (anti-HER2 CAR-M, autologous monocyte-derived) completed a first-in-human Phase 1 (Reiss et al., *Nat Med*, 2025): no dose-limiting toxicity, no severe CRS, TME remodeling and T-cell infiltration observed; the four stable-disease cases were all HER2 3+, while all HER2 2+ progressed — i.e. antigen-density-dependent, as theory predicts. The limitation is that macrophages **do not divide** and are short-lived, so dose is everything; this is why in-vivo generation (§4) is especially attractive for the myeloid chassis.

**Tregs** are the chapter's conceptual mirror (§3).

**HSCs/iPSCs** are the persistence/manufacturing chassis: an engineered HSC reconstitutes a lifelong supply of edited progeny, and iPSC-derived NK/T cells give a renewable off-the-shelf bank — at the cost of differentiation fidelity and (for iPSC-T) a still-immature potency profile.

### 2. Universal / adapter CARs — renting the kill engine

The cleanest decoupling of sensing from acting is the **adapter (universal/switchable) CAR**: the T cell expresses a receptor against an *inert bridge molecule*, and a separately dosed soluble **adapter** binds both the bridge and the tumor antigen. Genotype/antigen recognition lives in the adapter; the kill engine is generic. Families (reviewed in Arndt et al.; Cao et al., *Adv Drug Deliv Rev*, 2023):

- **Anti-FITC CAR + FITC-labeled antibody** (Tamada et al.; Unum/CAB platforms). Swap the antibody, retarget the same cells.
- **BBIR (biotin-binding immune receptor) CAR**: a dimeric-avidin ectodomain that clicks onto *any* biotinylated targeting molecule (Urbanska et al., 2012). In the absence of biotinylated adapter the cells are **inert** — a built-in off-switch.
- **UniCAR / "5B9" peptide-epitope tag** (Bachmann/Cartesian lineage): a short La/SS-B–derived peptide bridge; the adapter's very short serum half-life makes the system **rapidly reversible** — stop dosing, the CAR goes dark within hours.
- **SpyTag/SpyCatcher** covalent bridges (Minutolo et al.) and leucine-zipper "convertible" CARs.

**Why this is the load-bearing architecture for programmable medicine.** Three first-principles advantages: (i) **Dose-titratable & reversible** — CAR activity scales with adapter concentration, so toxicity (CRS, on-target/off-tumor) can be dialed down or switched off without killing the cells, a pharmacologic control knob that permanent CARs lack. (ii) **Multiplex & sequential targeting** — one cell product can hit antigen A today and antigen B next week (anti-escape), simply by changing adapters. (iii) **The synthesis with sense→tag.** If the *adapter* is replaced by a **genotype-induced synthetic surface tag** — the output of an ADAR/Cas13 variant sensor painting a non-human epitope only on cells of the right genotype — then a universal CAR becomes a **genotype-gated kill engine** with no clinical-grade re-engineering per indication. This is precisely the buildable v1b proposed in [genotype-directed cytotoxicity §5](../opto-car/seed-report.md): *the sensor installs the flag; the universal CAR rents itself to the flag.* The commercial corollary is that the effector becomes a **platform commodity** — one banked, well-characterized universal CAR-NK or in-vivo-generated universal CAR-T, monetized across an unbounded library of sensors/adapters.

### 3. Tregs and tolerance — killing run in reverse

The act layer is not only cytotoxic. The **regulatory T cell** is the same chassis wired to *suppress* rather than destroy, and it is the natural effector for autoimmunity, transplant tolerance, and any "turn the immune system **off** here" indication. A CAR-Treg homes via its CAR to a tissue antigen (e.g. HLA-A2 on a transplanted organ) and then exerts antigen-specific, *local* suppression through IL-2 consumption, IL-10/TGF-β, CTLA-4–mediated co-stimulation stripping, and direct cytolysis of antigen-presenting cells. Two emergent properties make Tregs uniquely powerful: **linked suppression** (suppression spreads to nearby antigens presented on the same APC) and **infectious tolerance** (recruited conventional T cells convert to *de novo* Tregs, propagating tolerance after the engineered cells are gone) — demonstrated for CAR-Tregs in islet transplantation (bioRxiv, 2024). Clinical exemplars: **Sangamo's TX200** (HLA-A2 CAR-Treg, renal transplant) and **Quell's QEL-001** (HLA-A2 CAR-Treg for liver-transplant tolerance), the latter a multi-module design with a **FOXP3 "Phenotype Lock"** (to prevent the notorious Treg→effector plasticity that would convert a suppressor into a killer) plus a safety switch; QEL-001 advanced into its LIBERATE Phase 1/2 efficacy cohort in 2024 with engraftment, allograft trafficking, and ~6-month persistence reported. The deep design principle: **Treg instability is the inverse of T-cell exhaustion** — both are epigenetic identity failures of an engineered effector, and both are now being engineered against (FOXP3 locks for Tregs; c-Jun and epigenetic reprogramming for killers, §5). Tolerance and cytotoxicity are two outputs of one programmable chassis.

### 4. In-vivo generation — collapsing the manufacturing bottleneck and COGS

The single biggest structural cost of autologous cell therapy is the **ex-vivo manufacturing loop**: apheresis → transduction → expansion → QC → lymphodepletion → reinfusion, taking weeks, costing six figures, and failing for the sickest patients whose T cells are too few or too exhausted to manufacture. **In-vivo CAR generation** deletes the loop by manufacturing the cell *inside the patient*. Two delivery physics, two emerging cohorts:

- **LNP-mRNA (transient).** A targeted lipid nanoparticle decorated with an anti-receptor antibody/scFv delivers CAR-encoding mRNA to T cells in situ; the CAR is expressed transiently (mRNA, no integration), an intrinsic safety and reversibility feature. **Capstan Therapeutics' CPTX2309** — a **CD8-targeted** tLNP delivering anti-CD19 CAR mRNA — entered first-in-human trials in 2025 for autoimmune disease, with NHP data showing CD8⁺ CAR-T generation, deep B-cell depletion, and naïve B-cell repopulation **without lymphodepletion**; **AbbVie agreed to acquire Capstan in June 2025** (deal valued up to ~$2.1B), a market signal that in-vivo CAR is now strategic. The originating science (Rurik et al., *Science*, 2022) showed transient mRNA CAR-T generated in vivo could reverse cardiac fibrosis — proof the approach generalizes beyond oncology.
- **Lentivirus (durable).** An engineered, T-cell- (or HSC-) targeted lentiviral particle integrates the CAR for durable expression. **Umoja Biopharma's VivoVec** generated CD20 CAR-T in non-human primates with B-cell depletion >10 weeks (published in *Blood*); **Interius BioTherapeutics' INT2104** (CD20-directed, generating CAR-T *and* CAR-NK) opened the **first-in-human in-vivo CAR trial (NCT06539338)** in 2024, with NHP data showing ≥75% B-cell reduction in 15/16 macaques; **EsoBiotec's ESO-T01** dosed its first multiple-myeloma patient and the company was acquired by **AstraZeneca (~$1B, 2025)**. Kelonia (Lilly) and Interius/Kite round out the field.

**Why this is the commercial inversion of the Part.** In-vivo generation does three things at once: (1) it **collapses COGS** from a bespoke per-patient biologics manufacturing run to a *vialed, off-the-shelf nanoparticle/vector* — a small-molecule-like supply chain; (2) it **removes lymphodepletion** (and its infection/cytopenia toxicity), widening the eligible population to autoimmune and even preventive indications; and (3) it makes the **effector a generic reagent** delivered by an address (the targeting ligand), so the same vialed product can, in principle, deliver *any* CAR/sensor cargo. Combined with §2's adapter logic, the end state is a **"kill engine on tap"**: a generically manufactured, in-body-generated effector whose specificity is supplied entirely by a separately programmable sensor/adapter — the autologous bottleneck dissolved, the unit of value shifted from the cell to the program.

### 5. Effector biology's hard limits — exhaustion, tonic signaling, and the payload tax

No effector is a perpetual-motion machine. The dominant failure mode of cytotoxic chassis is **exhaustion**: a progressive, epigenetically-fixed loss of proliferation, cytokine output, and killing, driven by chronic antigen stimulation and inhibitory-receptor (PD-1, TIM-3, LAG-3) accumulation. Worse, many CARs **exhaust themselves without any antigen** via **tonic signaling** — antigen-independent clustering of scFv ectodomains drives constitutive CD3ζ signaling. Lynn et al. (*Nature*, 2019) showed a tonically signaling, high-affinity GD2 CAR (the HA-28z construct, an E101K variant of GD2-28z) exhausts via scFv aggregation, with a transcriptional signature of AP-1 imbalance and chromatin opening at exhaustion loci; **overexpressing c-Jun rendered the cells exhaustion-resistant** and restored potency — a clean demonstration that exhaustion is a *programmable, druggable circuit state*, not an immovable fate. The design implications cascade through every effector decision:

- **Affinity and tonic load.** Higher-affinity, more-aggregation-prone scFvs signal harder *and* exhaust faster; the optimal CAR is detuned, not maximized.
- **Costimulation tunes durability vs. burst.** 4-1BB (CD137) CARs persist longer with lower exhaustion; CD28 CARs hit harder but burn out — a control-theory trade between gain and stability.
- **The payload tax (armored CARs / TRUCKs).** "**TRUCKs**" (T cells Redirected for Universal Cytokine-mediated Killing; Chmielewski & Abken, from 2011) carry an **NFAT-responsive IL-12 or IL-18** cassette so the cell secretes a pro-inflammatory cytokine *only upon CAR engagement* — a logic-gated "living micropharmacy" that recruits innate immunity and remodels the TME. Newer armored designs secrete checkpoint blockers, bispecific engagers, or dominant-negative TGF-β receptors. But every transgenic payload is metabolic and regulatory load: it competes for the cell's biosynthetic budget, risks systemic cytokine toxicity if the gate leaks, and can accelerate exhaustion. **Logic-gated secretion** (NFAT promoters, synNotch-driven payloads) is the discipline that keeps the micropharmacy local. The first-principles framing: the effector has a finite **energy/biosynthesis budget and a finite signaling-fatigue reserve**, and every added function (extra cytokine, extra gate, higher affinity) draws on both. Programmable medicine is in part the art of *spending that budget wisely* — which connects directly to the metabolic/epigenetic fitness assays in [universal functional potency](../cell-therapy-qc/universal-functional-potency-2026.md) and [the epigenetic-mitochondrial trajectory assay](../cell-therapy-qc/epigenetic-mitochondrial-trajectory-assay.md).

## Counterintuitive & Groundbreaking Applications

1. **The effector becomes a generic commodity rented by any sensor — the value migrates entirely to the program.** Stack §2 (adapter CAR) + §4 (in-vivo generation): a single, generically manufactured, in-body-generated universal CAR effector that reads a *programmable* tag. The defensible, monetizable IP is no longer the cell — it is the **library of sensors/adapters** that point the rented kill engine. This is the commercial thesis of the whole treatise (molecule→program) reduced to a supply chain: one effector SKU, an unbounded indication library. The genotype-painted-tag synthesis ([genotype-directed cytotoxicity](../opto-car/seed-report.md)) is the highest-value instance — a kill engine that any *intracellular* variant can rent.

2. **In-body manufacturing inverts the COGS and access curve.** If the effector is a vialed LNP/vector, cell therapy stops being a $400k bespoke biologic and becomes a *catalog reagent*. The counterintuitive consequence is that the economics flip toward **prophylactic and benign-disease use** (autoimmunity, transplant tolerance, even CHIP modulation) that the autologous price point forever excluded. AbbVie's $2.1B Capstan bet and AstraZeneca's ~$1B EsoBiotec bet are the market pricing this inversion in 2025.

3. **Macrophages as Trojan horses where T cells can't go.** CAR-M's phagocytose-then-present mechanism makes the killer *its own vaccine*: tumor antigens cross-presented after engulfment recruit a polyclonal endogenous T-cell response (epitope spreading), so a non-dividing, short-lived effector can seed a durable, broadening immunity it never directly mediates. The bold frontier is **in-vivo-generated CAR-M** — reprogramming tissue macrophages in situ — for solid tumors and fibrosis, where the infiltration problem that defeats CAR-T is the macrophage's home turf.

4. **Tolerance-as-a-service: Tregs as the inverse kill engine for the autoimmune/transplant market.** The same chassis, FOXP3-locked and CAR-targeted, sells *local immune silencing* with built-in amplification (linked suppression, infectious tolerance). Counterintuitively, the hardest engineering problem here is the *opposite* of exhaustion — preventing the suppressor from spontaneously converting into a killer — which is why phenotype locks, not signaling boosts, define the Treg design space.

5. **Serial-killing throughput is the under-exploited dosing lever.** Because ~1 in 5 CAR-T kills multiple targets, effector *quality* (synaptic off-rate, granule reload, metabolic fitness) can matter more than effector *number*. A potency assay that measures **kills-per-cell-per-hour** rather than cell dose (the [universal functional potency](../cell-therapy-qc/universal-functional-potency-2026.md) thesis) could let an order-of-magnitude smaller, fitter, in-vivo-generated dose match a large ex-vivo product — directly lowering toxicity and cost.

## Open Questions

- **Does in-vivo CAR generation achieve clinical durability and a clean safety profile?** Transient mRNA may under-treat malignancy (needs durable killing) while being ideal for autoimmunity; integrating lentivirus risks insertional mutagenesis in an uncontrolled in-body context. The risk/durability frontier per indication is unresolved (early human data from CPTX2309, INT2104, ESO-T01 pending).
- **Can the immune synapse actually concentrate energy/payload at the <10 nm scale required for next-generation contact-gated effectors?** The trans-cellular BRET / synapse-tether problem flagged in [genotype-directed cytotoxicity §15](../opto-car/seed-report.md) is the same physical question — what holds two membranes in Förster range, genotype-specifically — and it remains open for any optical or proximity-gated act layer.
- **How do we measure effector fitness predictively?** Exhaustion/tonic-signaling state is epigenetically encoded before it is functionally visible; release assays measure cell number and viability, not future serial-killing capacity. The trajectory/metabolic assays in the QC reports are an attempt, but no validated potency biomarker exists.
- **Is the off-the-shelf NK/iPSC persistence ceiling fixable, or is it intrinsic?** IL-15 armoring and memory-NK approaches extend persistence to ~weeks; whether allogeneic effectors can match autologous T-cell-years without re-introducing GVHD/rejection is unsettled.
- **For in-vivo generation, can targeting be made tight enough to avoid off-cell CAR expression** (e.g. mis-delivery to non-CD8 cells, or CAR-painting of the wrong lineage), and what is the immunogenicity of repeated vector/LNP dosing?
- **Treg phenotype stability under inflammatory stress:** do FOXP3 locks hold in a cytokine-storm microenvironment, or can a tolerizing product convert into an autoreactive killer?
